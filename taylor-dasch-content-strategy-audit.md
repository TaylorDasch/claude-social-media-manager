## 3. ALGORITHM & GROWTH INTEL

### YouTube Algorithm (Q1–Q2 2026)

**The February 2026 Browse Overhaul — The Most Important Change**

YouTube replaced broad topic categories in the Browse/Homepage feed with viewer watch history clusters — micro-niche groupings. According to [OutlierKit's February 2026 algorithm guide](https://outlierkit.com/resources/youtube-algorithm-updates/), this is the single biggest shift for small hyperlocal channels:

> "For creators, this is good news if you serve a specific niche. The algorithm is now better at finding the right viewers for focused content. Generic content that tries to appeal to everyone gets filtered out faster."

**For you specifically:** Every video you make about Belton ISD, BSW nurses, or out-of-state investors is getting served to viewers who have previously watched similar hyperlocal real estate content. The Browse feed has become a targeting engine. This is why your specialty videos outperform vague titles — it's not just CTR, it's audience match quality.

**The 5 Ranking Factors That Matter in 2026 (Ordered by Weight)**

| Factor | What It Means For You | Action |
|--------|----------------------|--------|
| **Viewer Satisfaction Score** (Very High) | Post-watch surveys, likes, shares, return visits | Deliver exactly what the title promises. Don't oversell. |
| **CTR** (High) | A/B test thumbnails monthly | Benchmark: 5%+ CTR is strong for real estate. Below 4% = thumbnail problem. |

| **Session Contribution** (Medium-High, increased 2026) | Does your video lead to another video? | End screens linking to related playlist (not just "subscribe") |
| **New Viewer Attraction** (Medium, new in 2026) | Are 50%+ of viewers new to your channel? | Track this in YouTube Studio; below 50% = discovery problem |

**Shorts Strategy in 2026**

As of late 2025, [YouTube fully decoupled the Shorts algorithm from long-form](https://outlierkit.com/resources/youtube-algorithm-updates/). Bad Shorts cannot hurt your long-form channel. Shorts now run at 200 billion daily views. For real estate, the winning Shorts format:

- **Length:** 30–45 seconds optimal (not 15s — too short to establish local credibility)
- **Hook format:** Start with the property or the number, not your face. "This $205K house has 4 bedrooms. Let me show you what you get."
- **Loop structure:** End on a cliffhanger or callback. "And the backyard? [end of video]" forces re-watch.
- **Posting cadence:** 3–5 Shorts per week, treated as a completely separate growth strategy
- **Best use case for you:** 30-second clips from property tours (Opus Clip auto-extracts), market stat reveals, "did you know" hooks about Temple TX data points

**What's Working for Small Channels (1K–10K Subs) Right Now**

Based on analysis from Channel Junkies (Jackson Wilkey), Ken Pozek's growth journey, and the [2026 Blueprint for Real Estate YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8FTFcnsErI):

1. **Map videos** — Screen-share tours using Google Maps or a custom-built map overlay. These retain viewers 30–40% longer than walking tours because viewers are cognitively engaged navigating with you. Zero channels in your geographic market are doing map videos for Temple/Belton/Killeen.

2. **"Vlog of the city" format** — Not polished studio content. Filming while driving through neighborhoods, at new construction sites, at the BSW campus parking lot during shift change. Authenticity outperforms production by 31% in retention metrics, per [2026 TikTok algorithm analysis](https://opus.pro/blog/tiktoks-new-algorithm-2026).

3. **Playlist architecture** — Every video should land in at least one playlist. Create playlists around: First-Time Buyers | New Construction | BSW/Medical | Military/Fort Cavazos | Neighborhoods Under $300K | Market Updates. This drives Session Contribution scores.

4. **Early CTA placement** — Move your CTA to appear between 90–120 seconds into the video (not at the end). Ken Pozek's data shows this placement converts at 3–4x the end-of-video CTA rate.

**Community Posts**

Post 2–3 times per week. Best formats:
- Market stat poll ("Temple TX median price just hit $270K — up or down by year end?")
- Behind-the-scenes photo of a property you're about to tour
- Link to your newsletter with a teaser quote
- "Which neighborhood should I cover next?" poll

**YouTube Premieres & Live Streams**

Ken Pozek built 65K+ subscribers with consistent weekly live streams covering the Orlando market. His model: Friday night live Q&A + market data review. For you, consider a biweekly live stream timed to your newsletter drop — "The Investor Brief Live Edition" — replaces one written newsletter per month with a video format, creates a live replay asset, and drives subscriber notifications.

### YouTube → Lead Conversion Benchmarks (2026)

**Lead Attribution System**

1. **Unique phone number per channel** — Use a forwarding number (Grasshopper, $26/mo) on each channel's channel art and description. Different numbers = clean attribution.
2. **UTM links in descriptions** — Every link to your website from YouTube includes `?utm_source=youtube&utm_campaign=living-in-temple` or `investing-in-temple`. Track in Google Analytics or your CRM.
3. **Intake form question** — "How did you find me?" with dropdown: YouTube (Living in Temple) / YouTube (Investing in Temple) / BiggerPockets / Referral / Other.
4. **Video-specific lead magnets** — Each major video links to a unique landing page ("Get the Temple TX Investor Calculator" vs "Get the Temple TX Relocation Guide"). You'll know which video generated which lead.

**Your $10/Day YouTube Ad Strategy**

At $300/month, you cannot run multiple campaign types. Focus 100% on one objective:

**Recommended Campaign Structure:**

- **Campaign type:** Video reach campaign (not leads — awareness is more efficient at this budget)
- **Ad format:** In-stream skippable (15–30 seconds) using your 3 highest average view duration videos
- **Targeting:** Custom intent audience based on Google searches for "moving to Temple TX," "Temple TX homes," "Austin relocation alternatives," "investing in Central Texas"
- **Geo-targeting:** NOT Temple TX — target Austin (78701–78759 zip codes), Dallas (75201–75230), Houston (77001–77099). These are your actual relocation feeder markets.
- **Exclusions:** Exclude your existing subscribers (they've already found you)
- **UTM tracking:** Every ad needs a UTM tag so you know which video as an ad generates site visits

**Scaling Trigger:** When a single video generates 3+ leads in one month organically, that video becomes your YouTube ad creative. Real estate YouTube ads run at $20–37 cost-per-lead when the creative is a high-retention organic video (not a produced ad), per [real estate YouTube ad analysis](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZzN78ZYKgGs).
