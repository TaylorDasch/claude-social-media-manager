## 1. Executive Verdict — Fastest Path to Qualified Leads

With TWO channels kept separate (corrected from original consolidation recommendation):

- **Living in Temple TX = weekly cadence, non-negotiable.** One long-form per week + 3 shorts per week. This is the lead-gen engine.
- **Investing in Temple TX = bi-weekly or event-driven.** Film investor content when you have a real deal to analyze, not on a calendar. One long-form every 2 weeks maximum, 1 short per week. Acts as top-of-funnel for BiggerPockets ($9K proven channel).
- **Unequal cadence is the solution.** Trying to post equally on both = the "half-weak, half-weak" trap. Weight the buyer channel because buyer YouTube search converts faster; let the investor channel run at a sustainable pace.
- **Anchor loop: "Neighborhood + Dollar Bracket" weekly series** on Living. Proven winner in Taylor's own data (Cimmaron 3.1x, Lake Pointe 2.6x).
- **Military househack = new high-ROI bucket on Investing.** Opens a massive underserved audience (young Fort Cavazos soldiers with BAH + VA loan).

**Meta-finding:** Your real problem is "consistency of deal flow," not "YouTube reach." The YouTube answer is evergreen Living videos that generate leads while you sleep — not investor content churn that educates your competitors.

## 7. Contrarian Section — What Could Make This Video Weak

### Overstatements to avoid
- "Belton schools are better than Temple schools" → they generally rate higher, but zoning matters more. Say that.
- "You'll love Belton because it feels like home" → agent-speak. Cut.
- "Temple is a real city and Belton isn't" → both are real. Frame by scale, not legitimacy.
- "You can't go wrong either way" → yes you can. Wrong school zone, wrong commute, wrong fit.

### Assumptions to challenge
- Viewer assumes property tax is uniform. It's not.
- Viewer assumes BSW commute is the only one that matters. Not for military or Austin commuters.
- Viewer assumes "small town = cheaper." Not true anymore for this pair.
- Viewer assumes resale favors bigger city. Not universally true.

### Acknowledge where Temple clearly wins
- BSW commute (5-10 min vs 15-25)
- Hospital-adjacent lifestyle (VA, specialists, ER proximity)
- Retail and restaurant density
- Specific premium neighborhoods (Alta Vista not replicable)
- Inventory volume
- Airport access (TPL)

### Cut/neutralize marketing-speak
- "Call me for a personalized..." → "PDF has the data, Calendly if you want to talk"
- "I love both cities" → neutral is better than love
- "Belton is a hidden gem" → banned word + generic
- Any phrase implying one city is universally better
- Any "dream," "charming," "turnkey," "nestled"

### Risk of inaccuracy
- ISD ratings shift yearly. Check before filming.
- Tax rates by zone change. Verify Bell County rates for both cities.
- New construction inventory and pricing shifts quarterly. Show current comps.
