## WALKTHROUGH SEQUENCE (2-4 min final cut)

Film as one continuous take if possible. If you cut between rooms, cut on doorway transitions.

### Shot 1: Exterior Front (0:00 - 0:15)
**Start:** Street-level, centered on the front of the home
**Move:** Slow walk up the driveway/walkway toward the front door
**Hold:** 3 seconds on front door before entering
**Note:** Show landscaping, garage, overall curb appeal

### Shot 2: Entry / Foyer (0:15 - 0:25)
**Move:** Push through front door, slow pan left-right to establish the space
**Show:** Crown molding, flooring transition, sight lines into living areas
**Hold:** Let the eye settle — don't rush past the entry

### Shot 3: Living Room / Great Room (0:25 - 0:45)
**Move:** Slow walk into the main living space
**Show:** Ceiling height, windows/natural light, flooring, fireplace if present
**Hold:** Wide establishing shot for 3-4 seconds
**Note:** If the living room connects to kitchen, let the camera naturally flow that direction

### Shot 4: Kitchen (0:45 - 1:15)
**Move:** Enter kitchen space, slow arc showing the full layout
**Show:** Granite counters, custom cabinetry, appliances (included in sale), island/bar if present
**Hold:** Pause on the counter/island — this is a money shot for buyers
**Detail shots:** Close-up pan across countertop, cabinet hardware, backsplash

### Shot 5: Dining Area (1:15 - 1:25)
**Move:** Natural flow from kitchen
**Show:** Size of the space, windows, any built-in features
**Hold:** Brief — 5-8 seconds

### Shot 6: Primary Bedroom (1:25 - 1:45)
**Move:** Walk in, sweep left-right to show size
**Show:** Window placement, closet doors, ceiling detail
**Hold:** Wide shot showing whole room, then walk toward bathroom

### Shot 7: Primary Bathroom (1:45 - 2:05)
**Move:** Enter from bedroom, slow pan
**Show:** Double vanity, shower/tub, tile work, mirrors
**Hold:** If the shower has tile detail or glass enclosure, give it 3 seconds

### Shot 8: Secondary Bedrooms (2:05 - 2:30)
**Move:** Quick but steady walk-through of each, 8-10 seconds per room
**Show:** Size, closets, windows, natural light
**Note:** Don't linger — buyers want to see them but the primary suite is the star

### Shot 9: Additional Bathrooms (2:30 - 2:45)
**Move:** Quick look into each additional bath
**Show:** Vanity, shower/tub, tile
**Note:** 5-8 seconds each is enough for secondary baths

### Shot 10: Garage (2:45 - 2:55)
**Move:** Open door from house, show the space
**Show:** Size (2-car?), storage, epoxy floor if present
**Note:** Quick — 8 seconds max

### Shot 11: Backyard — THE CLOSER (2:55 - 3:30)
**Move:** Walk out the back door, slow reveal of the backyard
**Show:** This is the "oasis" — patio, landscaping, any water features, covered area, fence line
**Hold:** WIDE shot for 5 full seconds — let it breathe. This is your closing impression.
**Detail shots:** Any special features — fire pit, outdoor kitchen, pergola, string lights
**Final shot:** Turn back toward the house, show the back exterior as the last frame

### Shot 12: Exterior Back/Side (3:30 - 3:45)
**Move:** Walk along the side yard back to the front
**Show:** Lot size perspective, any side features, back to curb appeal
**End:** Final hold on the home from the street — same angle you started, bookend it

## EDITING NOTES

- **Target:** 2-4 min clean
- **Music:** Soft instrumental, nothing distracting — buyer is mentally furnishing the house
- **Text overlay (first 5 seconds):** Address + Price + Beds/Baths/Sqft
- **No voiceover on MLS version** — silent with music. The video sells the space visually.
- **Transitions:** Cross-dissolve between rooms works for MLS. Keep it clean.
- **Upload:** YouTube (unlisted) or Vimeo → paste URL into MLS Virtual Tour field

---

## MLS COMPLIANCE NOTES

- Horizontal only (16:9) — never vertical
- No agent branding, logos, or contact info on the MLS version (check your MLS rules — some require it, most prohibit it)
- No music with lyrics (royalty issues)
- Show every room — don't skip anything
- No text overlays beyond address/specs on the MLS version (some MLS restrict marketing language in virtual tours)
