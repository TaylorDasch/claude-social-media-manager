## 3. PER-BUILDER SEGMENT STRUCTURE

### SEGMENT 1: D.R. HORTON (0:00-4:00) — "The Warning"

**Opening hook line:** "D.R. Horton is the biggest homebuilder in America. They build more homes than anyone. And there's a reason their BBB rating is 1.09 out of 5."

**3 Genuine Strengths:**
1. **Lowest entry price in Temple** — Pecan Creek starts at $213K. For a brand-new home with granite countertops, smart home package, and a privacy fence included, that's hard to beat on paper. (Show the model home — it LOOKS good.)
2. **Rate buydown is aggressive** — 3-2-1 buydown starting at 0.99% Year 1. On a $250K home, that's roughly $600/month less in Year 1 than market rate. For a military family on BAH, that math matters.
3. **They include sod, irrigation, and fencing** — Most builders charge extra for these. DR Horton includes full front/backyard sod with irrigation and a privacy fence. That's $15K-$20K you're not spending after closing.

**2 Honest Weaknesses:**
1. **DHI Mortgage is non-negotiable** — Every incentive (rate buydown, closing costs, flex cash) requires you to use their in-house lender. Their rates run .25-.50% higher than market. On a $300K loan, that "free" $7K in closing costs will cost you $36,000 in extra interest over 30 years. (Show the math on screen.)
2. **Warranty abandonment after closing** — DR Horton hands your warranty to a third-party insurer (RWC) after 24 months. The people who built your house are no longer responsible for it. With 12 of 14 construction superintendents leaving one division in a single year, there's nobody left who even remembers your house was built.

**The "hidden" thing:** The 2x4 exterior framing with basic R-13 fiberglass batt insulation is the minimum that passes code. In a Temple summer where it hits 105 degrees, your HVAC is working overtime to push cool air through walls that barely insulate. Your electric bill in July will be $100-150 more per month than a comparable home with spray foam or rigid foam insulation. Nobody tells you this at the model home because the model home's AC is always running.

**Best value pick:** If you insist on DR Horton, **Mesa Ridge in Belton ISD** ($274K-$331K) is the play. It's their nicest community, it's in the better school district, and the homes are single-story 1,500-2,000 sq ft — the sweet spot where their construction quality issues are less likely to compound (fewer stairs, simpler rooflines, less plumbing). But hire an independent inspector. Period.
