# Fryers Bend Luxury Tour — CREATIVE OPTIONS (FINAL)

> **Council recommendation:** Ship **Option 1** (Austin Two-Stage Split-Screen) + **Option 2** (104-Day Time-Lapse) TOGETHER. They fulfill the "one creative element" rule as two beats of the same honesty narrative. **HOLD Option 3** (Builder Head-to-Head) — relationship risk outweighs the payoff.

---

## Option 1 — Austin Two-Stage Split-Screen (PRIMARY — INTEGRATED)

### What It Is

A two-stage visual narrative device, NOT a single moment:
- **Tease at 1:45** — full-frame graphic: "WEST LAKE HILLS · $2.3M · 3,070 SQFT EQUIVALENT" (no Temple number yet)
- **Payoff at 3:50** — full-frame split-screen: West Lake Hills $2.3M ↔ Fryers Bend $765K (same sqft, same quality build)

### Why It Works

- **Directly hooks Austin-exit buyer persona** (the primary audience for the Living in Temple channel)
- **Shareable single-frame** — the split-screen frame is screenshot-bait for the Austin-to-Temple migration audience
- **Two-stage structure prevents the 2:30–4:00 retention valley** — most neighborhood tours sag there because the feature-list ramp is unbroken. The Austin tease reseeds attention at 1:45 before the valley, and the payoff lands at 3:50 — right as retention would otherwise crater
- **Validates the value proposition without political commentary** — we're not talking about Austin as a bad place to live; we're just showing sqft-for-dollar math
- **Honest framing** — we use "same sqft, same build quality" not "equivalent market" (West Lake Hills isn't functionally equivalent to Fryers Bend as neighborhoods — but $/sqft comparison holds)

### Cost / Effort

- **LOW effort** — Zillow / Redfin screenshots + After Effects overlay
- **No filming-day cost** — produced entirely in edit
- **Data refresh** — pull West Lake Hills 78746 median $/sqft within 48 hours of edit to avoid data drift
- **Time estimate:** 30–45 min in edit for both graphics

### Risk

- **Risk:** Some viewers will say "but West Lake Hills isn't Temple's actual competitor — Austin is way more than West Lake Hills"
- **Mitigation:** Script says "same sqft, same quality of build" — we're making a sqft/dollar comparison, not a market-equivalence claim. The "Not a typo. Not me pitching. That's the math." line owns the framing.
- **Additional mitigation:** Label the graphic "Source: Redfin public data" — transparent sourcing
- **Residual risk:** Low. This is a well-understood content genre (Austin-to-X comparisons) and the Redfin sourcing is standard.

## Option 2 — 104-Day Time-Lapse (SECONDARY — INTEGRATED)

### What It Is

At 5:05, instead of a static data overlay, a 15-second vignette:
- Visual: static camera on a real Fryers Bend for-sale sign (must be from a CLOSED home, not active)
- Overlay: "LISTED DAY 1 → DAY 30 → DAY 60 → DAY 90 → DAY 104 → SOLD 95.8% OF ASK"
- Small-text disclaimer bottom: "*median journey, 365-day MLS window*"
- Score: single slow piano note OR existing background music held through the moment

### Why It Works

- **Turns the honesty beat into the most shareable moment of the video instead of the weakest.** Most neighborhood tours bury DOM data in a static overlay — we make it a visual narrative
- **Trains the viewer that Fryers Bend rewards patience, not panic** — the visual arc of "Day 1 → Day 104 → Sold" normalizes the long timeline
- **Makes the "negotiating room" CTA earn itself visually** — when Taylor says "don't write full price on a home that's been sitting three months, you have room," the viewer has *just seen* the 104-day arc and the 95.8% closure
- **Most recallable moment at 24 hours** — the time-lapse visual is distinctive enough to be remembered, which means the video is forwardable

### Cost / Effort

- **LOW effort** — single static camera on a real Fryers Bend for-sale sign + edit overlay
- **Captured same filming day** — add 10 minutes to the schedule, that's it
- **Post-production:** 20–30 min for the time-lapse day-counter animation in After Effects

### Risk

- **Risk 1:** If the for-sale sign is from a LIVE (active) listing, the seller or listing agent may view the video as unauthorized commentary on their home/listing
- **Mitigation:** USE A CLOSED HOME ONLY. Pull MLS list of Fryers Bend homes closed in 2025, pick one where the sign is likely still physically in place (recent close), film static only.
- **Risk 2:** The 104-day number is a median, not this specific home's history
- **Mitigation:** On-screen disclaimer reads "median journey, 365-day MLS window" — honest and defensible. Script language also says "median days on market here is 104 days" — framed as a neighborhood aggregate, not this home's story.
- **Risk 3:** 95.8% is the list-to-sold ratio aggregate, not this home's sale
- **Mitigation:** Same — labeled "median."

## Option 3 — Builder Head-to-Head (HOLD — Do Not Use)

### What It Would Be

At 3:30, a 20-second visual comparison of Eagle Ridge Builders finish vs typical Temple production builder (D.R. Horton, Lennar, etc.):
- Iron door vs stock vinyl door
- GE Café range vs builder-grade appliances
- Quartz countertop vs laminate
- Custom cabinets vs builder-grade
- Paired with Taylor voiceover contextualizing "this is what $500/sqft buys you vs $150/sqft"

### Why It Was Considered

- Justifies the $765K tier for viewers who only know Temple as a $300K-production market
- Answers the unspoken question: "why is this worth 2x a production home?"
- Reinforces Eagle Ridge as the differentiator

### Why Council Rejected It (Hold)

- **Relationship risk with production builders.** Taylor has active referral channels with D.R. Horton, Lennar, and other production builders in the Central Texas market. Naming them on-camera in a comparison that positions them as "lesser" — even implicitly — could poison those channels over time.
- **Mitigation attempted — show contrast WITHOUT naming** — but still risks recognition: if D.R. Horton sees their render or floorplan in the comparison, relationship damage is possible.
- **The Austin split-screen + 104-day time-lapse together already over-deliver on the "one creative" rule.** Adding a third creative beat risks overloading the video and diluting the two beats that already work.
- **Opportunity cost:** Better to save the builder comparison for a dedicated "Eagle Ridge vs Production Builders in Temple" short-form piece or blog post, where the frame is direct and the relationship cost is consented to (or the frame is gentler and positioned as a buyer-education piece).

### When to Revisit

- When Taylor explicitly decides the production-builder referral channel is no longer strategically important
- When Eagle Ridge Builders grants a formal exclusive partnership that makes the comparison a consented sales asset
- When a dedicated "Builder Tiers in Central Texas" piece is planned where this content lives natively

## Taylor's Override Point

If Taylor wants a different single creative element instead of the two-beat narrative, here are the trade-offs:

- **Dropping Option 1 (Austin split-screen):** loses the Austin-exit audience hook and the 3:50 retention reseed — this would be a significant retention hit
- **Dropping Option 2 (104-day time-lapse):** keeps the Austin angle but makes the honesty beat visually weak; DOM 104 becomes a throwaway data point
- **Swapping to Option 3 (Builder head-to-head):** adds value for mid-video retention but trades off existing referral relationships

**Council conviction: ship both 1 and 2. The Austin angle is the audience; the 104-day visual is the memorable moment. Together they cover both the acquisition hook and the recall anchor.**
