## Production Rules (All 3 Shorts)

### DO
- Shoot native vertical on filming day — phone + gimbal, NOT the cinema camera
- Capture at least 3 takes per voice segment for flexibility
- Leave 0.5 seconds of motion before any voice — viewer must see movement before they hear
- Every Short loops cleanly (last frame ≈ first frame)

### DON'T
- ❌ No "Hi I'm Taylor" — not once in any Short
- ❌ No background music over dialogue — viewer commenter called this out on a prior video
- ❌ No "like and subscribe" endings — always unresolved question
- ❌ No repurposed YouTube-long-form clips — these are Short-native cuts

### Posting Sequence
1. **Day of long-form launch:** Short 1 (primary $40K angle) goes up same day
2. **Day +3:** Short 2 (Austin math)
3. **Day +7:** Short 3 (WFH niche)
4. **Day +10:** Start the next neighborhood's Shorts cycle

### Related Video Link (DO NOT SKIP)
After each Short publishes, go to YouTube Studio on DESKTOP, find the Short, click the "Related Video" feature and link to the full Fryers Bend long-form tour. This is the single highest Shorts-to-long-form conversion tactic and most agents miss it. Per retention research doc §6, rule 6.

### Caption Accessibility
- Burned-in captions preferred (auto YouTube caption is unreliable on aggressive cuts)
- Use CapCut or Premiere caption burn with high-contrast style
- Check mobile playback — captions must be readable on iPhone SE screen size
