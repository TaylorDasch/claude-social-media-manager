## Topic List — Every Beat the Video Must Hit

### Foundational Truths (6)
1. The builder narrative is easier than learning resale, but it costs you $50K–$100K you don't price in
2. Texas does not license home builders — anyone can advertise as a builder
3. The model home is a marketing tool, not a spec sheet
4. The base price is the worst lot, smallest plan, zero upgrades — $50K–$100K above sticker is the median real cost
5. The public inspector verifies code, not quality — independent inspector at every milestone is non-negotiable
6. Forced arbitration in your contract isn't there to protect you; the 2023 Texas Supreme Court ruling expanded arbitration's reach to subsequent owners — read page 9 before signing

### The Builder Tactics Buyers Don't Know — Verifiable Before Earnest Money (12)
1. **Captive lender spread** — DHI Mortgage rates run .25–.50% above market; "$7K closing credit" costs $36K over 30 years on $300K loan. Verify by pulling an outside-lender quote.
2. **Forced arbitration clauses** — typically page 9–14 of national builder contracts; Lennar v. Rafiei + Lennar v. Whiteley (TX 2023) expanded reach
3. **Liquidated damages cap** — builder's max liability is your earnest money refund, not actual damages. Read the cap clause before signing.
4. **Material substitution clauses** — "we reserve the right to substitute equivalent materials" — your hardwood becomes LVP, your granite becomes laminate
5. **Earnest money forfeiture conditions** — non-builder lender financing failure can void earnest money in some contracts
6. **Warranty Year-2 assignment to RWC** — your builder is no longer your point of contact; verify the assignment language at signing
7. **Model home dining-table removal** — staging trick to make the room look bigger than spec
8. **Kitchen island with no stools** — sells the size of the island; base spec is shorter
9. **Frameless shower in model, base glass in spec** — designed-up bathroom you don't get
10. **Closet built-ins in model, bare wall in spec** — closet looks fits-a-king-bed; your closet won't
11. **Lot premium not in base price** — $10K–$75K depending on view, corner, cul-de-sac
12. **Post-build essentials missing** — fence, blinds, refrigerator, ceiling fans, landscaping = $25K–$50K added after closing

### Temple-Specific Tips (9 — verifiable-now only)
1. **Bell County non-homestead = 2.366%** — verify on BellCAD before signing
2. **North Point Temple has an active PID** — pull the disclosure, confirm the dollar figure for the lot you're writing on
3. **Belton ISD vs Temple ISD zoning trap** — Belton ISD generally rates higher on TEA, but Temple has excellent zones; confirm specific zone before signing, not the district badge
4. **BSW commute by community** — Westfield/Canyon Creek 5–10 min; Atascosa Estates ~12–15 min; Mesa Ridge (Belton ISD) 15–25 min. Test it at 7:45 a.m. weekday before earnest money.
5. **Fort Hood commute by community** — Atascosa Estates and Mesa Ridge have the cleanest run on 190
6. **Mesa Ridge same-street walk** — D.R. Horton, Flintrock, and Carothers all build there. Best in-person trim/cabinet/hinge comparison in the market.
7. **Atascosa Estates builder mix** — Lennar / Dunhill / Smalley side-by-side; spec sheets read different even at similar sticker price
8. **Hartrick Ranch kitchen vent fan issue** — real but commonly misreported online as a "discontinued kitchen" — first-hand observation; confirm before letting an inspector flag the wrong thing
9. **Wind/hail insurance reality** — Texas margin gives high deductibles; pull a premium quote with named-storm and roof-age riders before contract

### Honest Negatives That Cost Builder Relationships (3 — all verifiable-now)
1. D.R. Horton's BBB rating is 1.09/5; warranty handoff to RWC at Year 2; captive-lender math is the $36K clause
2. Stylecraft's documented use of quarter-inch sheetrock and cut-in-half insulation in older inspection reports — biggest local volume, still won't recommend
3. Forced arbitration + 2023 TX expansion to subsequent owners — read page 9 before signing anything
