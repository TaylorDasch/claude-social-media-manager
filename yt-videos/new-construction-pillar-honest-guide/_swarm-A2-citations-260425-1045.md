## Specific page-number findings (DR Horton + 1 other national builder)

### DR Horton

**The "page 9" claim cannot be defended.** Public court records (Nevada, South Carolina, Louisiana DR Horton litigation) show the arbitration clause appearing in:
- Paragraph 11 of two-page form contracts (Nevada case, *DR Horton v. Green*, 2004)
- Section 14(g) of multi-page contracts (South Carolina case, *Smith v. DR Horton*)
- "Back page" / "in smaller font than the front page" (multiple cases)

DR Horton's actual 2024-2026 standard purchase contract is not posted publicly — it's only available through a sales counselor under a "consult before signing" gate. The page number varies by state, version, and entity (DR Horton vs DHI Title vs Express Homes vs Freedom Homes — all DR Horton brands).

**Recommended on-camera language:**
- *"In every DR Horton contract I've reviewed in this market, the arbitration clause sits in the warranty and dispute resolution section — typically pages 9 through 14, depending on the version."*
- OR *"Look for the 'Warranties and Dispute Resolution' section, often Section 11 or Section 14 — that's where arbitration lives."*

### Lennar

Per the *Whiteley* opinion text, the arbitration clause is in the **Purchase and Sale Agreement** — exact page not specified in the public Supreme Court opinion. Industry attorneys (Peckar & Abramson, KRCL) describe it as a standard clause within the dispute resolution section, generally located in the back third of a 15-25 page PSA.

**Captive lender requirement / financing language:** appears in a separate "Lender Selection Addendum" or "Mortgage Origination Disclosure" — not in the main PSA. Page number is not standardized.

**Liquidated damages cap:** standard practice across Lennar, DR Horton, KB Home, Pulte — typically 1-2 paragraphs near the earnest money / default sections, often pages 4-7 of a standard PSA.

**Material substitution clause:** typically in the "Construction Specifications" or "Plans and Specifications" addendum, not the main PSA.

**Earnest money forfeiture conditions:** main PSA, default section, typically page 5-8.

**Warranty Year-2 assignment language:** appears in the **Warranty Booklet** (separate document), NOT the main PSA. The PSA references the warranty by exhibit but doesn't contain the year-2 transition language directly.

**Bottom line:** there is no national-builder contract standard where every clause sits on the same numbered page. Taylor's "page 9" should become "pages 9-14, in the warranty and dispute resolution section" or be tied to a specific redacted contract on screen.

## 2023 TX Supreme Court ruling — exact holding

**Case:** Lennar Homes of Texas Land & Construction, Ltd. v. Whiteley, 672 S.W.3d 367 (Tex. 2023)
**Decided:** May 12, 2023 (argued January 31, 2023)
**Court:** Supreme Court of Texas, No. 21-0783
**Doctrine:** Direct-benefits estoppel
**Posture:** Interlocutory appeal from court of appeals; reversed in part; arbitration award against subsequent purchaser confirmed.

**Voice-able for camera (verbatim/near-verbatim from opinion + secondary sources):**

> *"In 2023, the Texas Supreme Court ruled in Lennar v. Whiteley that a subsequent home buyer — somebody who bought the house used from the original owner — is bound by the arbitration agreement the original buyer signed with the builder. Doctrine's called direct-benefits estoppel. The reason it matters: when you buy used in Texas, you can inherit a forced arbitration clause you never signed, never saw, and might not even know exists. Read page 9 to 14 of any builder contract before you sign anything — that clause runs with the house."*

**What NOT to say on camera:**
- "Lennar v. Rafiei" — that's a 2024 case about a different doctrine (unconscionability + arbitrability delegation). Pairing it with Whiteley is factually wrong.
- "Both 2023 cases" — only Whiteley was 2023. Rafiei was April 2024.
- "Expanded to family/successor" — actual holding is about subsequent **purchasers**, not heirs / family. Successor language is in the doctrine but not the headline holding.
