## Round 1 — Framing

### 1. Algorithm Strategist (AS)

**Proposed framing:** *"The 5-second hook is the whole video. Lead with a redacted contract page held to the lens, a dollar number on screen, no greeting — and the body has to deliver that exact dollar number with proof. CTR + first-30-seconds retention is 80% of this video's success."*

**Defense:** Retention Rabbit data: video that breaks 50% retention = top 16.8% of all videos = 3.5x faster subscriber growth. The pillar's biggest risk isn't body content — it's that the hook is too soft. A 13:30 runtime fights length bias (10–20 min bucket = 26% retention floor). The only way to earn 13:30 is a hook that *threatens money* in the first 5 seconds.

**Critique of CC:** Contrarian Critic wants to "name names" of walked-away builders. That tanks first-60-second retention — viewers haven't earned the trust to absorb it. Saturated trope from Phoenix/Florida creators. Bury it at 8:30, after the contract clause earns the credibility.

**Tradeoff:** Hook tension = cost. If we lead with "$36K trap," viewers who don't relate to that exact stakes-frame bounce. Acceptable cost — better to filter for the 60-min relocator than chase a casual viewer who won't convert.

### 2. Buyer Advocate (BA)

**Proposed framing:** *"Walk the actual contract on camera. Page 9 forced arbitration. Page 11–14 captive lender. Page 17 material substitution. Page 22 liquidated damages. The video IS the contract review most buyers never get because they don't have an attorney."*

**Defense:** Every buyer who signs in Temple this year is signing 50–80 pages they don't read. Builder agents count on it. The single highest buyer-trust signal Taylor can produce is to physically hold a redacted contract on camera and walk through specific clauses. AEO bonus: "page 9" + clause language = exact AI citation surface.

**Critique of CE:** Conversion Engineer wants to spine the video around the Builder Scorecard funnel. That's right architecturally, BUT if every Scorecard line item is a sales beat, the body collapses into infomercial. Each Scorecard point must cite the actual contract clause behind it — content first, funnel second.

**Tradeoff:** Specificity = legal exposure if Taylor names the wrong page or clause. Mitigation: redact contract pages, use generic builder language ("most Texas builder contracts include..."), mark unverified specifics `[VERIFY BEFORE FILMING]` and pull a fresh DR Horton or Lennar contract the morning of.

### 3. Local Authority (LA)

**Proposed framing:** *"The same-street walk: Mesa Ridge has DR Horton + Flintrock + Carothers on the same street. Atascosa Estates has Lennar + Dunhill + Smalley. North Point has Omega. No agent in Phoenix or Houston can film this, period. The pillar's spine is the community-by-community walk."*

**Defense:** Strategy doc rule: "If you could film it from Austin reading Zillow, cut it." The same-street comparison is the moat. Three builders, identical lots, identical micro-neighborhood, 30-min film, instantly disqualifies any non-local creator from competing. Taylor walks Mesa Ridge with score-cards in real time.

**Critique of AS:** Algorithm Strategist's $36K hook is solid, but if the body is a generic "5 contract clauses" lecture, the video could have been filmed in Phoenix. Every body segment has to anchor in a specific Temple community Taylor has walked or closed in — otherwise the moat evaporates and we're competing with national creators.

**Tradeoff:** Filming constraint — Mesa Ridge same-street drone needs HOA permission or carefully-timed off-hours. Cost to verify access and film windows: 1 dedicated half-day before main shoot. If access fails, fall back to dashcam drive-by + on-camera narration.

### 4. Contrarian Critic (CC)

**Proposed framing:** *"Three builders I've walked my own buyers away from — and why. Name them. The one positive thing each one does. The one disqualifying thing. If every builder in Temple still wants to work with Taylor after this video drops, the video wasn't honest enough."*

**Defense:** Buyer-trust is built or broken in the segment Taylor's the most scared to film. National builders don't refer Taylor business — local agents/lenders do. So the "relationship cost" of naming D.R. Horton's BBB rating, Lennar's arbitration, Centex's Pulte Mortgage rating is *zero*. The cost is to Taylor's *perception of being safe*, not to any actual referral pipeline. Safe = forgettable.

**Critique of LA:** Local Authority's same-street walk is structurally great, but if the on-camera narration is "all three of these builders have things to consider" — that's diplomatic mush. The same-street walk has to end with Taylor saying "I'd let a client buy this one. I'd walk a client away from this one." Period.

**Tradeoff:** Reputational risk if a builder takes legal exception. Mitigation: every claim cites a public source (BBB, ConsumerAffairs, court docket, builder's own contract language). Phrase as buyer-advocate: "Here's what the BBB shows" not "this builder is bad." Keep one positive per builder named to manage proportionality.

### 5. Cross-Market Researcher (CM)

**Proposed framing:** *"Adapt Matt Risinger's Build Show 'on-camera scoring' device + Jackson Wilkey's 'what I'd tell my sister' tone-shift segment. Score every builder against the 12-point Scorecard out loud, on the lot, while walking. Then sit-down for the walked-away segment. Two devices, both proven in other markets, neither used in Temple."*

**Defense:** Build Show's verbal scoring drives re-watches and pause/screenshot events (retention engine). Jackson Wilkey's "sister" voice-shift drives trust signal at exactly the slump zone (55–65%). These are devices that survive in any market because they're voice-delivery patterns, not formats. They make the body visually and tonally varied without runtime bloat.

**Critique of CE:** Conversion Engineer wants three CTAs — pre-roll, mid-roll, end-roll. That's correct on architecture, but if every CTA is "grab the Scorecard," the wording becomes wallpaper. Each CTA needs a different angle: pre-roll = credential ("same one I use"), mid-roll = utility ("easier with it open"), end-roll = next-step ("DM me if you want it via DM instead").

**Tradeoff:** Importing devices risks Taylor sounding *like* Risinger or Wilkey, not like himself. Mitigation: Taylor's substrate (closed buyers, walked communities, Bell County tax math) makes the device Temple-specific even if the structure is borrowed. The voice filter is the safety check.

### 6. Conversion Engineer (CE)

**Proposed framing:** *"The Builder Scorecard PDF IS the video. The 12 Scorecard points are the body's spine; every body segment is one Scorecard point in story form. The pillar exists to produce one outcome: Builder Scorecard PDF in the inbox of every Temple new-construction buyer in 2026. Funnel architecture is the framing."*

**Defense:** Lead magnet pairing is locked (strategy doc). Builder Scorecard PDF is the deliverable. Every other framing is in service of getting the PDF downloaded. The video's ROI is measured in Scorecard download rate × conversion-to-Calendly rate × Calendly-to-listing rate. The framing must put the PDF at the structural center, not at the end as an afterthought.

**Critique of BA:** Buyer Advocate's "walk the actual contract" is gold for content but doesn't answer "what does the viewer leave the video doing?" Reading a contract on camera makes a buyer scared, not equipped. The Scorecard makes them equipped. Each body segment ends with "and that's why this is point [N] on the Scorecard."

**Tradeoff:** Funnel-spined videos can feel sales-y if every segment is a CTA reminder. Mitigation: only three explicit CTAs (pre-roll, mid-roll, end-roll); the other 11 minutes of body is content, not pitch. The Scorecard appears as a *prop on camera*, not as a verbal sales beat, in 4–5 places between CTAs.

This is the framing Taylor would actually open with on camera.

### Disagreements logged

**Issue 1: Body length vs hook-economy.**
- AS argued: 13:30 too long, target 11:30, audience drops by 12:00.
- LA argued: pillar tier earns 13:30 if interleaved with pattern interrupts.
- **Decision:** 13:30 with pattern interrupt every 60–90 sec required.
- **Why it won:** AS's data point (10–20 min bucket = 26% retention floor) is real; LA's counter is real (pillar earns runtime if pattern-interrupted). Hybrid wins. CC vetoed shortening if it cuts walked-away segment.
- **What got cut:** No segment cut. Pre-roll Quick CTA capped at 10s exactly (down from suggested 15s). Body 4 PID/MUD compressed from 60s to 45s.
- **What got marked `[VERIFY BEFORE FILMING]`:** Bell County 2.366% rate, North Point current PID dollar exposure.

**Issue 2: Walked-away segment placement.**
- AS argued: bury at 11:00+ to protect first-half retention.
- CC argued: place at 8:30 — the slump zone. Honest negatives ARE the retention engine in slump zone.
- **Decision:** 8:30–10:30. CC wins.
- **Why it won:** Retention research explicitly says pattern interrupts fix the 55–65% slump. The walked-away segment IS the pattern interrupt — tone shift, sit-down, voice drops, eye contact tightens. It pulls viewers through the slump rather than past it.
- **What got cut:** Plan to embed full 30-min contract review as final segment — runtime collapse. Spun out as Follow-Up Video #2.
- **What got reframed:** Two of three walked-away builders — names redacted to "I won't name them on camera, but if you want to know, comment NEWBUILD or DM me." (Manages relationship cost; trust signal preserved through one verified named claim — the D.R. Horton BBB 1.09 + captive lender math which is fully cited in Top3 Bible.)

**Issue 3: Scorecard prop placement.**
- BA argued: don't show the PDF until end-roll, otherwise viewers download it and leave.
- CE argued: show it on tablet at mid-CTA (6:45) so the back half of the video is "easier to follow with it open in another tab."
- **Decision:** CE wins. Show PDF at mid-CTA.
- **Why it won:** Save-rate is a stronger algo signal than completion rate at this length. Viewers who download AND watch back half = top-quartile engagement. Viewers who download AND leave = still good (lead captured). Net positive either way.
- **What got cut:** "Exclusive" / "insider" framing for the PDF — banned word violation. Reframed to "the same one I use."
- **What got marked `[VERIFY BEFORE FILMING]`:** Builder Scorecard PDF must be built and live at templetxhomes.net/builder-scorecard before publish.

**Issue 4: Mesa Ridge filming permission.**
- LA argued: same-street walk requires HOA + listed-builders coordination.
- AS argued: don't gate the pillar on coordination logistics; film dashcam-only if access fails.
- **Decision:** Pre-shoot Mesa Ridge same-street drone scene 1 week before main shoot. Confirm walk-on access. If denied, fallback = dashcam drive-by + on-camera narration from sidewalk.
- **Why it won:** LA's moat point is real, AS's risk-management is real. Pre-shoot reduces risk without sacrificing moat.

**Issue 5: Mid-CTA verbal wording.**
- CE proposed: "Pause here. Grab the Scorecard at the link below — the next six points are easier with it open."
- CC pushed: "Don't soften — viewers should download because they NEED it, not because it's a nice-to-have."
- **Decision:** Hybrid — "Pause here. Grab the Scorecard. The next six points are about contract clauses, model-home staging, and community-by-community reality. Worth having open."
- **Why it won:** CE's utility framing + CC's no-softening = stronger CTA. "Worth having open" replaces "easier with it open" — shifts from convenience to necessity.
