## Honest dissent

The Council-validated Hook A scores 22/30. Variant 3 scores 25/30. That's a real but moderate edge. Hook A is already a strong hook — number-first, audience-filtered, locality moat strong, voice on-brand.

The reason Variant 3 wins:
1. **Lower retention risk.** Hook A makes the viewer wait 55 seconds for the captive-lender clause reveal. Variant 3 puts the proof (PID disclosure) on screen at 0:02. In a 13:30 pillar where Taylor's channel leaks hardest at 20–30 seconds (per his retention data), a hook that proves itself before 30 sec materially out-retains a hook that promises and delays.
2. **Higher locality moat.** $36K is a Temple-specific number, but the visual ($-text overlay on a sidewalk walk) is replicable by any creator. North Point gate + actual PID disclosure document is unfakeable.
3. **Title pair strength.** Variant 3's title at 53 chars leaves room. Hook A's title at 67 chars is at the ceiling.

**Where Hook A still wins:** The script.md production package, the Council validation lineage, and the title backup ladder are all built around the $36K number. Switching to Variant 3 means re-anchoring the cold-open identity drop, the mid-CTA, and the Body 1 → Body 3 pivot. That's not free.

**Honest take:** If Taylor has already built shoot logistics around Hook A and the cost of switching is a half-day of repackage, ship Hook A and A/B test the thumbnail with Variant 1's `1.09/5` BBB number instead. If he hasn't shot yet and shoot day can include both Mesa Ridge and North Point, ship Variant 3 as primary and Hook A as the swap test.

The wildcard variant (Variant 5 — split-screen rate sheets) is the highest-leverage idea I came up with. It scores 23 — within 1 point of the winner — and the proof in the first frame (two real quote sheets) is the most defensible against a remix-this-hook-with-my-numbers competitor.
