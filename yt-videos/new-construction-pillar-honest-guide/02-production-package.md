## 7. Topic List — Every Beat the Video Must Hit

### Foundational Truths (7)

1. The builder narrative is easier than learning resale — but it costs you $50K–$100K in invisible upgrades, captive-lender spread, and post-build essentials
2. Texas does not license home builders — code inspection ≠ quality inspection
3. The model home is a marketing tool, not a spec sheet — the house you sign for is on the spec, not the model
4. The base price is the worst lot, smallest plan, zero upgrades — what's advertised is rarely what's built
5. The municipal inspector verifies code; the independent inspector you hire works for you
6. Builder warranty year 2+ is typically assigned to RWC; the people who built the house no longer answer your warranty calls
7. Forced arbitration clauses (per 2023 Texas Supreme Court) have expanded reach — `[VERIFY BEFORE FILMING]` exact statutory language before naming "binds family/successor"

### The Builder Tactics Buyers Don't Know (12)

1. **Captive lender spread** — DHI Mortgage and similar preferred lenders run 0.25–0.50% above market; on a $300K conventional 30-yr, that's $36K over the life — `[VERIFY BEFORE FILMING]` current spread vs outside lender quote
2. **Forced arbitration clauses** — typically located on contract pages 9–14
3. **Liquidated damages cap** — builder's max liability is your earnest money refund — `[VERIFY BEFORE FILMING]` current Texas builder contract language
4. **Material substitution clauses** — "we reserve the right to substitute equivalent materials" — common in TX builder contracts
5. **Earnest money forfeiture conditions** — non-builder lender failure can void earnest money in some contracts — `[VERIFY BEFORE FILMING]` current language
6. **Warranty assignment to RWC at year 2** — verified pattern in national builders
7. **Model home dining table removed** — staging trick that makes room read bigger
8. **Kitchen island with no stools** — staging trick that hides spec
9. **Frameless shower in model, base glass in spec** — common upgrade-blindness
10. **Closet built-ins in model, bare wall in spec** — visible-but-not-built upgrade
11. **Lot premium not in base price** — $10K–$75K typical add for view/corner/cul-de-sac — `[VERIFY BEFORE FILMING]` current Mesa Ridge / Atascosa lot premium ranges
12. **Post-build essentials** — fence, blinds, refrigerator, ceiling fans, landscaping = $25K–$50K added after closing — `[VERIFY BEFORE FILMING]` current Temple-specific cost range
