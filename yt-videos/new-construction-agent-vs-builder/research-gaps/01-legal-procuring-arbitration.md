## 1. Procuring Cause: What Actually Happens When a Buyer Registers Without an Agent

### Finding 1 — Texas practitioner consensus (documented, repeated across sources, NOT a single case)

Every Texas source surfaced said the same thing in near-identical language: if a buyer registers at a builder's model home without an agent (or without having their agent call ahead and pre-register them), the builder is generally not obligated to add the agent to the deal later, even if the buyer has a signed Buyer Representation Agreement with that agent.

- **Texas-specific source (HAR.com):** "The builder does not have any obligation to pay a buyer's agent that was not with the buyer on the initial visit." | Source: https://www.har.com/question/3304_can-a-home-builder-decline-to-add-buyer-agent-to-the-purchase-agreement | accessed 2026-04-17 | [secondary — practitioner Q&A]
- **NewHomeSource Professional (industry-standard guidance):** "an agent may pre-register the prospective buyer at the builder's sales office prior to the prospective buyer's first visit to the community and must accompany the prospective buyer to the community within 48 hours of the prospective buyer's first visit." | Source: https://learn.newhomesourceprofessional.com/how-to-register-your-buyer-with-a-builder/ | accessed 2026-04-17 | [secondary]
- **Texas Real Estate Research Center (TAMU):** "Commission Mythology 101" explains that builders themselves frequently claim procuring cause when a buyer walks in and registers without an agent, because their advertising, model home, and on-site rep are the causal chain. | Source: https://trerc.tamu.edu/article/commission-mythology-101-2373/ | accessed 2026-04-17 | [primary — Texas housing research authority]

**Why this matters for the video:** This is the *rule*, not an edge case. Every single Texas source confirms the mechanic.

### Finding 2 — *Lennar Homes of Texas Inc. v. Rafiei* (Tex. 2024, No. 22-0830)

This isn't a procuring-cause case — it's the Texas Supreme Court binding a Lennar homebuyer to mandatory arbitration even for personal-injury claims. Included here because it shows what unrepresented buyers later discover they signed away.

- **Facts:** Mohammad Rafiei bought a new Lennar home in Texas. Approximately three years after purchase, "there was a sudden and unexpected explosion" of the garbage disposal when he turned it on, injuring him. He tried to sue in Texas court.
- **Outcome:** Texas Supreme Court (April 5, 2024) held Rafiei's unconscionability challenge failed because he didn't produce enough evidence that arbitration costs were prohibitive. He was forced into private AAA arbitration.
- **$ impact:** Rafiei's attorneys argued AAA arbitration costs exceeded $100,000 for a consumer plaintiff. The Court found this insufficiently documented on the record.
- **Contract language (directly extracted from Tirios SEC exhibit 63 of a Lennar purchase agreement, which uses the same base template Lennar uses nationally including Texas):** Arbitration was "administered by the AAA in accordance with the AAA's Construction Industry Arbitration Rules."
- **Source:** https://law.justia.com/cases/texas/supreme-court/2024/22-0830.html | accessed 2026-04-17 | [primary — Texas Supreme Court opinion]
- **Secondary coverage:** Gibson Dunn: https://www.gibsondunn.com/texas-supreme-court-holds-arbitrator-must-decide-questions-of-arbitrability-absent-evidence-that-delegation-clause-is-itself-unconscionable/ | accessed 2026-04-17 | [secondary]

### Finding 3 — *Lennar Homes of Tex. Land & Construction, Ltd. v. Whiteley* (Tex. 2023, No. 21-0783)

- **Facts:** Subsequent (second) buyer of a Lennar home in Texas tried to sue for construction defects. Whiteley never signed the original arbitration agreement — she bought the house from the first owner.
- **Outcome:** Texas Supreme Court held that the arbitration agreement ran with the land. Even a subsequent, unrepresented buyer who never saw or signed Lennar's original contract was bound to arbitrate.
- **$ impact:** Not quantified in the opinion; case sent back to arbitration.
- **Source:** https://law.justia.com/cases/texas/supreme-court/2023/21-0783.html | accessed 2026-04-17 | [primary — Texas Supreme Court opinion]

### Finding 4 — *D.R. Horton* Louisiana moldy-homes litigation (Dixon family, 2022–2025)

- **Facts:** Dixon family (Youngsville, LA) bought a new D.R. Horton home. They alleged severe mold and HVAC defects, claimed D.R. Horton knew its ventilation systems were defective. Sales contract included a mandatory arbitration clause on the back page.
- **Outcome:** Louisiana First Circuit Court of Appeals ruled December 2025 that the arbitration clause was unenforceable for these plaintiffs. Case proceeded in state court. Homeowners' counsel (Murphy Law Firm, Unglesby & Macy) argued D.R. Horton "schemed to keep buyers from taking legal action by tricking them into signing sales contracts with buried arbitration clauses."
- **$ impact:** No settlement announced as of March 2026. Class certification sought.
- **Source:** https://rightfuladvice.com/dr-horton-lawsuit/ | accessed 2026-04-17 | [secondary — consumer-law aggregator]
- **Primary source on arbitration denial:** https://www.theadvocate.com/acadiana/news/courts/article_703de8b0-2252-11ed-a950-6f803fa28508.html | accessed 2026-04-17 | [secondary — news, but reporting on ruling]

### Finding 5 — *DR Horton, Inc. v. Green* (Nev. 2004, 120 Nev. 549, 96 P.3d 1159)

Older but foundational. Cited repeatedly in subsequent arbitration challenges.

- **Facts:** Nevada buyers signed D.R. Horton purchase agreement. Alleged construction defects. Arbitration clause was on back page in fine print.
- **Outcome:** Nevada Supreme Court ruled the arbitration clause was procedurally unconscionable because it "failed to indicate that by agreeing to binding arbitration, the homebuyers were giving up significant rights under Nevada law, and the clause was in fine print and indistinguishable from many other contractual provisions." Clause void.
- **$ impact:** Not stated; case remanded.
- **Source:** https://law.justia.com/cases/nevada/supreme-court/2004/40102-1.html | accessed 2026-04-17 | [primary — Nevada Supreme Court opinion]

## 2. Actual Arbitration Clause Language (Verbatim)

### Lennar (verbatim — source: SEC-filed exhibit of actual Lennar purchase and sale agreement, Tirios Inc. Form 1-A Exhibit 6.3)

**Arbitration clause:**
> "The parties to this Agreement specifically agree that it is their desire to efficiently and quickly resolve any disputes that arise, that this transaction involves interstate commerce, and that any Dispute (as hereinafter defined) shall first be submitted to mediation and, if not settled during mediation, shall thereafter be submitted to binding arbitration as provided by the Federal Arbitration Act (9 U.S.C. §§1 et seq.) and not by or in a court of law or equity."

**Jury trial waiver (verbatim, as filed — caps in original):**
> "BUYER ACKNOWLEDGES THAT JUSTICE WILL BEST BE SERVED IF ISSUES REGARDING THIS AGREEMENT ARE HEARD BY A JUDGE IN A COURT PROCEEDING, AND NOT A JURY. BUYER, SELLER, AND BUILDER AGREE THAT ANY DISPUTE, CLAIM, DEMAND, ACTION, OR CAUSE OF ACTION SHALL BE HEARD BY A JUDGE IN A COURT PROCEEDING AND NOT A JURY. BUYER, SELLER, AND BUILDER HEREBY WAIVE THEIR RESPECTIVE RIGHT TO A JURY TRIAL."

**Class action waiver (verbatim, as filed — caps in original):**
> "THE PARTIES AGREE THAT BUYER, SELLER, AND BUILDER MAY BRING CLAIMS AGAINST THE OTHER ONLY ON AN INDIVIDUAL BASIS AND NOT AS A MEMBER IN ANY PURPORTED CLASS OR REPRESENTATIVE ACTION OR COLLECTIVE PROCEEDING."

**Rights waived:**
- Right to a jury trial (explicit)
- Right to participate in a class action or representative proceeding
- Right to have disputes resolved in a court of law or equity
- Mediation must occur before arbitration
- AAA Construction Industry Arbitration Rules apply (per Rafiei opinion)

**Source:** https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1975188/000147793223007344/tirios_ex63.htm | accessed 2026-04-17 | [primary — SEC-filed Lennar purchase agreement exhibit]
**Corroboration:** Lennar Homes of Texas Inc. v. Rafiei, No. 22-0830 (Tex. Apr. 5, 2024) confirms Lennar's Texas agreement uses the Federal Arbitration Act and AAA Construction Industry Arbitration Rules.

### D.R. Horton (partial — verbatim full text could NOT be sourced)

Multiple published rulings confirm that D.R. Horton's Texas and multi-state purchase agreements contain a **mandatory binding arbitration provision on the back page** with **class-action waiver** language. The exact phrasing, however, is not published in consumer-accessible form — it is quoted only fragmentarily in court opinions:

- Courts describe the clause as requiring "any disputes be resolved through a private arbitration system" with the buyer waiving "the right to go to court and instead requires that any disputes be resolved through a private arbitration system." (D.R. Horton Louisiana litigation, KPEL-FM, 2025)
- In court filings, attorneys describe D.R. Horton's clause as "buried" and "in fine print and indistinguishable from many other contractual provisions" (per *DR Horton v. Green*, 120 Nev. 549).
- **Rights waived (per practitioner summaries and court findings):** jury trial, class action, broad scope ("any disputes"), fine-print placement (back page).

**Source (fragments):** https://caselaw.findlaw.com/court/fl-district-court-of-appeal/2142664.html | https://law.justia.com/cases/nevada/supreme-court/2004/40102-1.html | https://rightfuladvice.com/dr-horton-lawsuit/ | all accessed 2026-04-17 | [primary court opinions + secondary news]

### Centex / Pulte (warranty waiver verbatim — arbitration clause not sourced)

**Warranty waiver (verbatim, caps in original):**
> "At closing Seller will deliver to Purchaser, Seller's standard form of homeowner's Limited Home Warranty against defects in workmanship and materials, a copy of which is available to Purchaser. PURCHASER AGREES TO ACCEPT SAID HOMEOWNER'S WARRANTY AT CLOSING IN LIEU OF ALL OTHER WARRANTIES, WHATSOEVER, WHETHER EXPRESSED OR IMPLIED BY LAW, AND INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO THE IMPLIED WARRANTIES OF GOOD WORKMANLIKE CONSTRUCTION AND HABITABILITY. PURCHASER ACKNOWLEDGES AND AGREES THAT SELLER IS RELYING ON THIS WAIVER AND WOULD NOT SELL THE PROPERTY TO PURCHASER WITHOUT THIS WAIVER."

Source: Centex warranty disclaimer quoted in *Centex Homes v. Buecher*, 95 S.W.3d 266 (Tex. 2002) | https://caselaw.findlaw.com/court/tx-supreme-court/1258125.html | accessed 2026-04-17 | [primary — Texas Supreme Court opinion]

**Rights waived:** all implied warranties, including the implied warranty of habitability and good workmanlike construction. (Note: In *Buecher*, the Texas Supreme Court held this waiver was **unenforceable** as against public policy for the habitability component, but the *good workmanlike construction* waiver can be enforced with sufficient specificity. Still an active contractual ask from Centex/Pulte.)

**GAP:** Centex/Pulte arbitration clause verbatim text not sourced. Confirmed present but unquoted in public filings.

## 3. Addendum Stack Page Counts (Texas Comparison)

### TREC 1-4 Family Residential Contract (Resale) — the Texas promulgated baseline

- **Current version (TREC Form No. 20-18, effective January 3, 2025):** **11 pages** total (form footer reads "Page X of 11")
- **Previous version (20-17):** 11 pages
- **Older versions (e.g., 2011):** 9 pages
- **Source:** TREC official form at https://www.trec.texas.gov/forms/one-four-family-residential-contract-resale and TREC form PDF | accessed 2026-04-17 | [primary]

Note: Existing deep-research file (dated April 10, 2026) states "9 pages of promulgated form" — that was the older version. Current is **11 pages**.

### Lennar purchase and sale agreement stack (Texas)

From Lennar's SEC-filed exhibit (Tirios filing, 2023):
- **~11 pages main agreement** + multiple addenda/riders including:
  - Rider B (Austin/San Antonio Division)
  - Master Disclosure and Information Addendum
  - Affiliated Business Arrangements Disclosure Statement
  - Cooperating Broker Agreement
  - Purchase Price and Payment Addendum
  - Addendum — Natural and Manmade Products

  - Insulation Addendum
