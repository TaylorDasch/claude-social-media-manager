### The variance story (Taylor's video hook)

On the same Antelope Trail cul-de-sac in Atascosa Estates, **two builders are selling a 1,600-sqft, 3-bedroom, 2-bath single-story home BOTH NAMED "THE ROBIN."** Dunhill's Robin lists in the $261,990–$290,990 range. Ameritex's Robin starts "in the $200,000s." That's potentially a **$30K–$90K spread on the exact same floor-plan name, same square footage, same basic layout** — without an agent walking both models and comparing spec sheets, the buyer has no idea which is the better value.

On top of that: Lennar's 1,269-sqft Ramsey move-in-ready is priced at $269,990 ($212.76/SF) in Watermill Collection 2, while Dunhill's 1,600-sqft Robin at $261,990 = ~$163/SF. A buyer walking in through Lennar's sales office would pay **$50/SF more** for a smaller house than if they'd walked into Dunhill's office one street over — and Lennar's on-site rep has zero duty to disclose that.

**Key finding — the disclosure gap is real.** Bell CAD's MUD #1 and MUD #2 district pages are both **locked behind login walls** ("You must be logged in to view this content"). That means even a diligent buyer trying to do homework on Three Creeks Belton before signing a D.R. Horton contract cannot find the MUD tax rate online without calling Bell CAD Belton office at 254-939-5841. **This is the single strongest Temple-specific story for the video.**

### Disclosure-gap talking points

- **Texas Property Code §5.014 requires MUD notice before contract execution** (covered in existing research). In Three Creeks Belton, a buyer contracting a D.R. Horton Colt Creek home has to be handed a MUD disclosure — but the disclosure is a form, not a dollar amount on Zillow, and the actual MUD #1 rate isn't freely searchable on Bell CAD's website.
- **Legacy Ranch is the "clean" example** — fully annexed into Temple city limits, no MUD, no PID, just a $550/year HOA and city services. Arnold Design+Build and John Houston Homes buyers get the simplest tax stack in the market.
- **Three Creeks is the "trap" example** — Stillhouse Lake amenity story dominates builder marketing, but the Bell County MUD #1 tax is a structural add-on that compresses the affordability story once added to Belton ISD's $1.1494 rate. A buyer qualifying at the edge of their loan is the one who gets hurt most.

### New 2025-2026 Texas lawsuit signals (flagged)

- **D.R. Horton:** W.D. Texas fair-housing-disability complaint; active March 2026 mortgage-practices class action with no settlement reached (property-tax-estimate deception allegations).
- **Lennar:** Schwarz v. Lennar Homes LLC (Florida, class cert pending Jan 2026); CalAtlantic/Lennar structural-defect class filed April 2024 still active; **Texas Supreme Court 2023 Lennar v. Whiteley** expanded arbitration enforcement — even family members and subsequent owners bound (existing research already captured this, but it's now fully cited case law).
- **Meritage:** Roanoke TX Briarwyck foundation-repair non-disclosure complaint surfaced 2026.
- **Texas Residential Construction Commission remains sunsetted** (2009) — builders still unlicensed in Texas.
