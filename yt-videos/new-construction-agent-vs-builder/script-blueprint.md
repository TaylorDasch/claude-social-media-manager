## 6. FIVE QUESTIONS A BUYER'S AGENT ASKS THAT A SALES REP NEVER WILL

Each question is designed to be quotable, specific, and positioned as something the builder does not want asked.

### Question 1 — The Net Effective Price
> "What is the net effective price after every incentive, rate buydown subsidy, closing credit, and design center credit is stripped out? Show me the number without the financing package."

**Why it's sharp:** Isolates the real price. Exposes the gap between "advertised monthly payment" and "appraisal-supported value at resale."

### Question 2 — The Comp Check
> "Show me the three most recent closings in this section of the community — address, square footage, and closed price per square foot — not list price, not model-home price, closed price."

**Why it's sharp:** Texas is a non-disclosure state. Only MLS-participant agents can pull verified closed-price comps. The sales rep often can't answer this.

### Question 3 — The MUD/PID Disclosure
> "What is the current MUD or PID assessment on this lot? I want the district name, the tax rate per hundred of assessed value, the bond payoff year, and the per-year dollar impact on a $350,000 assessed value. Before I sign anything."

**Why it's sharp:** Texas Property Code Section 5.014 requires disclosure, but it is routinely buried. The question forces a written disclosure before earnest money.

### Question 4 — The Appraisal Clause
> "If the home does not appraise for contract price, what does the contract require me to do? Can I walk away with my earnest money, or am I required to bring cash to closing to cover the gap? Point me to the exact paragraph."

**Why it's sharp:** The D.R. Horton and Lennar answer is "you bring the cash or you forfeit." The sales rep will not volunteer this.

### Question 5 — The Arbitration Carve-Out
> "Is the arbitration clause mutual — does it bind the builder to arbitrate any claim it has against me, including its own lien rights and mechanic's lien rights? Or is it one-sided? And can I cross the jury waiver before I sign?"

**Why it's sharp:** Most builder arbitration clauses are one-sided — buyer arbitrates, builder preserves lien rights. An unrepresented buyer never asks this.
