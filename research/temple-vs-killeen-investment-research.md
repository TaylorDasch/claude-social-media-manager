## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Temple and Killeen sit 30 minutes apart in Bell County, Texas. Both benefit from Fort Hood and the I-35 corridor, but they are fundamentally different investment markets. Temple is a healthcare/logistics economy with diversified employers and stronger appreciation trajectory. Killeen is a military-dependent economy with lower entry prices and higher gross yields but more tenant risk. Harker Heights is Killeen's upscale sister city with higher price points and better tenant quality.

## 4. FORT HOOD BAH RATES — THE MILITARY RENTAL FLOOR

### 2026 BAH Rates (Fort Hood, TX)

| Rank | With Dependents | Without Dependents |
|------|----------------|-------------------|
| **E-5 (Sgt)** | $1,695/mo | $1,530/mo |
| **E-7 (SFC)** | $2,070/mo | $1,668/mo |
| **O-3 (Captain)** | $2,340/mo | $1,833/mo |

**2026 increase:** Fort Hood BAH rose 6.3% from 2025 (national avg was 4.2%).

**BAH Arbitrage Play:**
- An E-7 with dependents receives $2,070/mo in BAH. A 3BR home in Killeen rents for $1,400-$1,600. The soldier pockets $400-$670/mo.
- An O-3 with dependents at $2,340/mo could rent in Harker Heights at $1,600-$1,700 and pocket $640-$740/mo.
- BAH sets a predictable rent floor. Even if market rents dip, military tenants pay based on BAH, not market comps.

**The BAH advantage for investors:** Military tenants are guaranteed income. If your Killeen rental is priced at or below BAH, you will always have demand. The risk is vacancy during PCS (Permanent Change of Station) cycles — typically June-August when thousands of families rotate in and out simultaneously.

5. **2,000 Acres Shovel-Ready** — Temple has massive industrial development capacity.

2. **DOD Barracks Overhaul** — $1.2B nationwide barracks initiative announced. Fort Hood is a likely beneficiary as the largest Army post.
3. **Population Growth** — Killeen's 164K population is nearly 60% larger than Temple's 103K. Sheer size means more rental demand volume.
4. **I-14 Corridor** — Same infrastructure benefits as Temple — the highway connects both cities.

## 8. RISKS & DOWNSIDES

### Temple Risks

1. **Higher Entry Price** — $261K median means more capital required per door. Harder to cash-flow.
2. **Higher Property Taxes** — Temple ISD's $1.1372 rate is the highest school district rate in the comparison. On a $250K home, you pay ~$650/yr more than Killeen ISD.
3. **Price Correction Underway** — Down 4-9% YoY. Could continue softening before stabilizing.
4. **103 Days on Market** — Longer DOM means less liquidity if you need to exit quickly.
5. **New Construction Competition** — 410+ new homes for sale puts downward pressure on existing home prices and rents.

### Killeen Risks

1. **Military Dependency** — Fort Hood IS Killeen's economy. A BRAC round (Base Realignment and Closure) would be catastrophic. The last BRAC was 2005. Congress has discussed excess military infrastructure but hasn't authorized a new round.
2. **Crime Perception** — Killeen's violent crime rate is 542 per 100,000 — 46.4% above the national average. Safer than only 17% of Texas cities. This isn't just perception — it affects tenant quality, insurance costs, and resale.
3. **Tenant Turnover** — Military PCS cycles create near-guaranteed turnover every 2-3 years. Budget $2,000-$4,000 per turn (paint, cleaning, minor repairs).
4. **Property Damage** — Anecdotally and statistically, military-heavy rental markets see higher property damage rates. Younger tenants (E-3 to E-5, ages 19-25) with guaranteed income but less experience maintaining homes.
5. **Price Stagnation** — Killeen has been flat to negative on appreciation for over a year. Limited upside beyond cash flow.
6. **Population Uncertainty** — The 2025 population estimate controversy (157K vs 162K+) raises questions about whether Killeen's growth has stalled.
7. **Rising Vacancy** — Metro vacancy at 9.7% is well above the 4.8% low of 2022.

### Harker Heights Risks

1. **Highest Entry Price** — $322K median. Hard to make cash flow math work.
2. **Still Military-Dependent** — Nicer neighborhoods, but the economic engine is still Fort Hood.
3. **Thin Margins** — 6% gross yield with higher price point means slim or negative cash flow.
4. **Limited Exit Liquidity** — Smaller market with fewer buyers at the $300K+ price point in this area.

## 9. THE "WINNER" FRAMEWORK

### Best for Cash Flow: KILLEEN

**Why:** Lowest entry point ($227K), highest gross yield (7.9%), BAH-backed rents, lower property taxes than Temple. If you can buy below median at $180-200K and rent at $1,400-$1,500, Killeen produces the best monthly cash flow of the three markets.

**Caveat:** Higher turnover, more property damage, and crime concerns eat into net returns.

### Best for Appreciation: TEMPLE

**Why:** $700M data center, $110M manufacturing plant, BSW expansion, I-14 freeway conversion, Austin spillover, 3.1% population growth rate (3x Killeen's rate). Temple's diversified economy supports long-term price appreciation. The current dip (-4 to -9%) is a buying opportunity, not a trend reversal.

**Caveat:** You need to be patient. Appreciation plays require a 5-10 year hold minimum.

### Best for BRRRR: KILLEEN

**Why:** More distressed inventory at lower price points. A $150K fixer that ARVs at $220K with $40K rehab is findable in Killeen. The spread between distressed and retail is wider. Rent supports the refi at 75% LTV if you buy right.

**Caveat:** Appraisals in Killeen can be conservative. Make sure your ARV assumptions are supported by recent comps within 0.5 miles.

### Best for Buy-and-Hold Rental: TEMPLE

**Why:** Diversified tenant pool (healthcare, logistics, industrial), lower turnover, better tenant quality, stronger appreciation trajectory. Temple's economy doesn't depend on a single employer. Total return (cash flow + appreciation + principal paydown) favors Temple over a 10-year hold.

### Best for Section 8: KILLEEN

**Why:** Waitlist closed since 2017 with 3-5 year wait. Extreme demand. FMR of $1,470 for 3BR in core Killeen. If you buy a $180K home and rent to a voucher holder at $1,400-$1,470, your rent is government-guaranteed. Killeen's lower price points make the Section 8 math work better.

### Best for MTR (Medium-Term Rental): TEMPLE

**Why:** BSW residency match (100-150+ new residents every June), year-round travel nurse demand, higher per-diem/housing stipends than military TDY. A furnished 3BR near BSW can command $2,200-$2,800/mo on a 3-6 month lease vs $1,700 long-term.

### Best for BAH Arbitrage: KILLEEN (but Harker Heights is the sweet spot)

**Why Killeen:** Lower rents mean soldiers pocket more BAH. An E-7 with dependents gets $2,070 but can rent a solid 3BR for $1,500 — pocketing $570/mo. This keeps Killeen rentals in perpetual demand from military families.

**Why Harker Heights is the sweet spot:** Better neighborhoods, lower crime, better schools perception, and BAH is the same rate (based on Fort Hood zip, not city). An O-3 family getting $2,340 BAH will choose a $1,600 Harker Heights home over a $1,400 Killeen home — they want quality of life. You get a better tenant, less damage, less turnover, and they're paying with government-guaranteed income.

## 10. TAYLOR'S TAKE — THE BOTTOM LINE

**If you have $60K to invest and want cash flow NOW:** Buy a $200K fixer in Killeen, rehab it for $30K, rent it to a military family at $1,400-$1,500/mo backed by BAH. DSCR loan at 6.5%, 75% LTV. You'll cash flow $100-200/mo after all expenses. Not life-changing, but the military guarantees your rent.

**If you have $80K and want total return over 10 years:** Buy in Temple. A $260K home near BSW renting at $1,700 might break even monthly, but Temple is adding $800M+ in new economic development (data centers + manufacturing). When those jobs arrive, rents follow. You're buying Temple's future.

**If you want the best of both worlds:** Buy in Harker Heights. You get Fort Hood BAH-backed tenants, lower crime, lower turnover, and Killeen ISD tax rates. The price is higher ($320K+), so the cash flow is thinner, but your total cost of ownership (less turnover, less damage, less vacancy) often beats a "cheaper" Killeen property.

**The move nobody talks about:** Buy a Temple property near BSW and run it as an MTR (furnished, 3-6 month leases) targeting travel nurses and medical residents. You can gross $26K-$34K/year vs $20K long-term — a 30-70% revenue increase. Temple's BSW pipeline is the most underexploited niche in this entire market.
