## 1. YOUTUBE REAL ESTATE VIDEO SEO (2026)

### Title Formulas That Work RIGHT NOW
| Formula | Example for Today's Open House |
|---------|-------------------------------|
| **[Price] + What You Get + [City]** | "What $389K Gets You in Temple TX (Full Tour)" |
| **[Negative Hook] + [Location]** | "This Temple TX Home Has One Problem Nobody Talks About" |
| **[Specific Number] + Outcome** | "$389K | 4 Bed Open House Tour — Temple TX" |
| **[Event] + [Property]** | "OPEN HOUSE: Walking Through Every Room at [Address] Temple TX" |
| **[Price Comparison]** | "$389K in Temple TX vs $389K in Austin — You Won't Believe the Difference" |

**Title rules:**
- Always include "Temple TX" — front-load if possible
- Under 60 characters for full mobile display (70 max before truncation)
- Front-load the most interesting word: number, negative, or specific detail
- Year-stamp if time-sensitive: "(March 2026)"
- Affordable/price-specific titles consistently outperform generic ones (confirmed by your own channel analytics)

### Description Strategy (7-Section Structure)
1. **Answer-first paragraph (FIRST 2-3 LINES — CRITICAL)** — These appear above "Show More" and are what AI engines extract. Include: address, price, beds/baths, one standout feature, your entity declaration. Example: "Tour this 4-bedroom home at [Address] in Temple, TX listed at $389,000. I'm Taylor Dasch with EG Realty — $27M+ in sales, 100+ transactions. Here's what makes this property stand out and what you need to know before buying."
2. **Context paragraph** — Who this home is for (BSW medical, Fort Hood military, investor, family)
3. **Timestamps** — Every section break, formatted as questions matching your page H2s. YouTube chapters appear in search results and create structured data AI can parse.
4. **Areas/neighborhoods mentioned** — Link to templetxhomes.net pages
5. **Key data points as text** — Redundant with video, intentional for AI parsing (price, taxes, HOA, school ratings, commute times)
6. **About Taylor** — Boilerplate credentials
7. **Contact + links** — templetxhomes.net, Calendly, socials

**Primary keyword must appear within the first 25 words** — critical for both mobile visibility and AI parsing.

### Tags Strategy
Tags carry less weight than they used to but still matter for:
- Alternate spellings (Temple Texas, Temple TX)
- Niche discovery (real estate investing Texas, Fort Hood homes)
- Use 8-12 tags: mix of broad + specific + location
- Example set: `Temple TX homes for sale, Temple TX real estate, [neighborhood] Temple TX, homes under $400K Temple, Fort Hood housing, BSW Temple TX, Bell County real estate, open house Temple TX, [address]`

### Thumbnail Strategy (2026 Data)
- **Real estate is one of the highest-CTR verticals on YouTube** — capitalize on this
- Good CTR from search: 8-15%. Good CTR from browse: 3-7%. Top performers: 8-12%
- **Expressive faces increase CTR by 20-30%** — your face showing genuine reaction to a feature (NOT the "agent pointing at house" trope)
- High contrast: bright text on dark background (your brand colors work perfectly: emerald text on midnight)
- Maximum 4-5 words on thumbnail
- Property photo + overlaid price number = highest CTR for listing tours
- **Use YouTube's built-in A/B thumbnail testing** — upload 2-3 variations per video
- Human authenticity: genuine micro-expressions beat AI-generated thumbnails by 22% in long-term click satisfaction

**Thumbnail concepts for today:**
1. Your face (surprised expression) + hero exterior shot + "$389K" in emerald
2. Split frame: best interior feature + price overlay
3. Drone shot of property + "OPEN HOUSE" text + price

### Ideal Length
- **YouTube listing tour: 3-7 minutes** (sweet spot is 4-5 minutes for a standard home)
- Detailed tours with data/analysis: up to 8-10 minutes if retention architecture is solid
- Under 3 min feels thin; over 10 min and you lose casual viewers
- **Key: retention rate matters more than length.** A 5-min video with 60% retention crushes a 12-min video with 30%

### AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) for Video
This is the 2026 differentiator. AI engines now cite YouTube as a primary source.

**Critical stats:**
- YouTube is cited **200x more than any other video platform** in AI-generated search answers
- YouTube + Reddit account for ~60% of all social citations in AI answers
- 44.2% of ChatGPT citations come from the first 30% of content
- Content formatted for LLM extraction is **3x more likely to be cited**

**How to optimize:**
1. **State the question clearly in the first 10 seconds** — "What does $389K get you in Temple, TX?"
2. **Deliver the answer early** (first 30-60 seconds) — don't save the punchline
3. **Organize content into focused sections** that each explain one idea
4. **Chapter titles as questions** — "What school district is this in?" "What are the property taxes?" "What are the negatives?"
5. **Accurate captions/transcripts** — AI reads your transcript, not your video. Manual cleanup for proper nouns (BSW, Fort Hood, Bell County, neighborhood names)
6. **Repeat key data points in description text** — redundant with the video, intentional for AI parsing
7. **Entity declaration first 10 seconds** — "I'm Taylor Dasch with EG Realty in Temple, TX" (establishes E-E-A-T)

## 2. INSTAGRAM REELS ALGORITHM (2026)

### What Changed
- Instagram's 2026 redesign puts **Reels front and center** — it's now the primary content format
- Hashtags have shifted from "growth levers" to "filing labels" — they categorize but don't drive discovery
- Posts WITHOUT hashtags achieved **23% higher reach** than hashtag-heavy posts in recent studies
- The algorithm prioritizes **completion rate** over everything else

### Ideal Length
- **15-30 seconds**: Quick hooks, single-point tips, price reveals — highest completion rates
- **30-90 seconds**: Tutorials, storytelling, mini-tours — the sweet spot for property content
- **1-3 minutes**: In-depth education (rarely needed for listing content)
- **Rule of thumb:** A 10-second Reel with 80% retention beats a 60-second Reel with 30% retention every time

### Hook Timing (THE Critical Factor)
- **Up to 50% of viewers drop off in the first 3 seconds**
- Reels with strong 3-second hold rates (>60%) outperform weak holds (<40%) by **5-10x in total reach**
- Your first frame must have: bold visual, compelling text overlay, or engaging movement
- **Never start with your name, logo, or brand intro** — hook first, always

### Caption Strategy
- Write keyword-rich captions (Instagram uses NLP to understand and distribute content)
- Keep CTA simple and consistent: "DM TOUR" or "DM INFO" across Reel, caption, and Stories
- Use the caption for context the video doesn't provide (price, address, neighborhood stats)
- Front-load the most interesting line (only first 2 lines show before "...more")

### Hashtag Strategy (2026 Reality)
- **3-5 relevant, specific hashtags** — not 30 generic ones
- Think of them as category labels, not growth tools
- For today: `#TempleTexas #TempleRealEstate #OpenHouse #HomeTour #TexasHomes`
- Focus your energy on strong captions, text overlays, and trending audio instead

### Music/Audio Strategy
- **70% trending audio / 30% original audio** is the balanced mix
- Look for the upward arrow next to audio names while scrolling — indicates currently trending
- Audio trends start on TikTok and migrate to Instagram within 1-2 weeks
- For property tours: ambient luxury music (no lyrics) works for silent luxury POV tours
- For talking-head hooks: original audio with your voice performs better than music overlays
- **For listing tours specifically:** trending instrumental audio under a fast-paced tour, or your voice with light background music for analytical content

### Posting Cadence
- 3-5 Reels per week for maximum algorithm favor
- Reels for reach + fast discovery, Carousels for saves/shares from serious buyers, Stories for urgency

## 3. TIKTOK REAL ESTATE VIRAL PATTERNS (2026)

### Formats Going Viral RIGHT NOW

**1. Silent Luxury POV Tours**
- Walk through the home with NO talking — just footsteps, doors opening, ambient luxury music
- Works because it's immersive: viewer imagines living there without being sold to
- Best for high-end features (kitchen reveal, primary bath, backyard)
- Film on gimbal with slow, deliberate movements

**2. Speed Tours**
- Fast-paced, energetic walkthroughs — entire house in 15-30 seconds
- Humor and personality optional but helps
- Music-driven with beat drops on room transitions
- Works great for mid-range homes where you want to show value quickly

**3. Price Reveal / "What $X Gets You"**
- Hook: "Here's what $389K gets you in Temple, Texas"
- Walk through showing features while the viewer mentally compares to their own market
- **The Math Hook is the highest-converting hook for 2026** — not "Just Listed" but "Here's what $2,400/month gets you in Temple TX"
- Show comps and rationale for price

**4. "Wait For It" Drone Reveals**
- Start inside the home (nice but normal) → walk to backyard → cut to drone pullback revealing the full property/view/pool/lot
- The reveal is the payoff — structure the entire video building toward it
- Works best when the property has one standout exterior feature

**5. Before/After or Day-to-Night**
- Time-lapse of the property from setup to open house event
- Show the staging, the prep, the crowd arriving
- Creates narrative arc in 15-30 seconds

**6. Price Comparison Geography**
- "$389K in Temple TX vs $389K in Austin" — split screen or sequential
- Drives engagement from comments ("I'm moving to Temple!")
- Geographic comparison content consistently goes viral

### Hook Best Practices for TikTok
- **3-5 seconds max** — shorter than YouTube
- Bold statement on screen as text overlay even if someone watches on mute
- Lead with the most jaw-dropping feature
- Show the front of the house or a walkthrough WHILE delivering the hook
- **Never start with your name or brand**
- State target keywords verbally in first 3 seconds AND mirror in on-screen text

### Caption Strategy
- Keyword-rich captions so TikTok pushes to the right buyers
- Include city, neighborhood, price range, property type
- 5-10 hashtags using the 3-tier system: Tier 1 (broad: #realestate), Tier 2 (mid: #texasrealestate), Tier 3 (niche: #templeTX #forthood)
- End with DM keyword CTA: "Comment TOUR for details"

### Posting
- 2-3 videos per week minimum for visibility
- Save to TikTok drafts natively, publish manually — third-party schedulers get depressed reach
- Geo-tag EVERY post: Temple, Bell County, Texas

## 4. DRONE + CINEMA CAMERA FILMING TECHNIQUES

### Sony A7SIII Settings for Interior Cinematic Footage
| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Resolution** | 4K (XAVC S 4K) — allows cropping to 1080p if needed |
| **Frame Rate** | 4K/24fps for cinematic, 4K/50fps for slow-mo B-roll |
| **Picture Profile** | S-Log3 for high-end edit flexibility OR Cine4 for faster turnaround |
| **Aperture** | f/4 in bright rooms, f/2.8 in darker rooms. f/8 for deep-focus establishing shots |
| **ISO** | Auto ISO (80-100,000) — especially useful panning between dark/bright rooms |
| **Shutter Speed** | Double your frame rate (1/50 for 24fps, 1/100 for 50fps) |
| **Lens** | Wide-angle 16-35mm f/4 OSS — stabilization matters more than fast aperture on gimbal |
| **Steady Shot** | ON - Active when on gimbal, OFF on tripod |

### Gimbal Shot Types for Interior
1. **Dolly In** — Slow push into each room (the money shot)
2. **Dolly Out** — Reverse pull from a feature (fireplace, kitchen island)
3. **Tracking** — Follow a path through hallways, capturing flow
4. **Crane** — Bend knees for vertical movement (low to high reveal of vaulted ceilings)
5. **Orbit** — Partial arc around kitchen island or dining table (use sparingly)
6. **Ninja Walk** — Smooth heel-to-toe movement, essential technique for gimbal work

### Drone Shot Sequence (Exterior/Aerial)
Execute in this order for efficiency:

1. **High Establishment Shot (200-250 ft)** — Broad community/neighborhood context, slowly approach property
2. **Medium Parallax (100-125 ft)** — Arc around the property, front and back
3. **Close Parallax (50 ft)** — Tighter orbit showing roof condition, yard, pool
4. **Top-Down / Bird's Eye** — Straight down showing lot size and layout
5. **Low Reveal** — Start behind a tree line or fence, rise to reveal the property
6. **Pullback** — The "wait for it" shot: start tight on property, pull back to show full context

**Tip:** Capture ALL drone footage first (weather/light dependent), then move inside for the A7SIII work.

### iPhone 16 Pro Vertical Content Strategy
The iPhone is your **social-first camera** — everything shot here is ready for TikTok/Reels without reformatting.

**Settings:**
- Shoot in 4K/30fps (Cinematic mode optional for shallow DOF shots)
- Use ProRes if storage allows — better color grading flexibility
- Lock exposure before starting each clip (tap and hold)

**What to capture on iPhone (vertical, 9:16):**
1. POV silent luxury walk-through (gimbal-mounted iPhone)
2. Quick "What $389K gets you" speed tour (handheld, energetic)
3. Face-to-camera hooks at the front door, kitchen, and backyard
4. Before/during open house transformation clips
5. Reaction shots at standout features
6. Stories content: behind-the-scenes setup, "we're live" moments

### Efficient Multi-Platform Shoot Order (One Visit, All Content)

**Phase 1: Drone (15-20 min) — Do First**
- All 6 drone shots above
- Capture during golden hour if possible (morning open house = great light)
- These shots serve: YouTube intro/outro, IG Reel openers, TikTok drone reveals

**Phase 2: Sony A7SIII Interior Cinematic (30-45 min)**
- Gimbal walk-through of entire home, room by room
- Slow, deliberate movements — you'll speed up in editing for social cuts
- Get 2-3 passes: one full continuous walk-through, one with pauses at hero features, one with alternate angles
- Serves: YouTube full tour, hero shots for all platforms

**Phase 3: iPhone Vertical Content (15-20 min)**
- POV silent tour (gimbal-mounted)
- 3 face-to-camera hooks at different locations (front door, kitchen, backyard)
- Speed tour (handheld, walking fast, personality on)
- Feature reveals: "wait for it" moments at best features
- Serves: TikTok, IG Reels, YouTube Shorts, Stories

**Phase 4: Open House Event Content (During Event)**
- iPhone Stories: crowd arriving, conversations (permission!), "happening now" energy
- Quick face-to-camera recap: "We just had 15 groups through in the first hour"

- **Instagram Story countdown:** Use the countdown sticker — followers get notified when it hits zero

### During the Open House (Live Content)
**Instagram Stories (post every 30-45 min):**
- "We're live at [address] until 4pm — come by!" with panorama of the living room
- Golden-hour light flooding a room (if applicable)
- Crowd social proof: if busy, show it — "Third group in the first hour!"
- Quick feature highlight: "This kitchen island... come see it in person"

**Facebook/Instagram Live (optional, 5-10 min):**
- Walk-through while people are there — social proof of interest
- Answer live questions about the property
- Creates urgency: "I've already had three people ask about making offers"

**TikTok "Happening Now":**
- Quick vertical clip: "OPEN HOUSE happening RIGHT NOW at [address] in Temple TX"
- Show the energy, the setup, the visitors (with permission)
- Post between 11am-1pm for maximum same-day reach

### Post-Event Content (Same Day or Next Day)
- **Recap Reel:** "Here's what happened at Saturday's open house" — montage of best moments
- **Data drop:** "[X] groups came through, [Y] showing requests, still available at $389K"
- **Urgency post:** "If you missed Saturday's open house, here's what you missed" + best clips
- **YouTube upload:** Full cinematic tour (filmed during Phase 2-3 above) — no need to wait

### Urgency Hooks That Work
- "This won't last long — come see it before it's too late"
- "Already had [X] showing requests after the open house"
- "Happening NOW until [time] — [address] in Temple TX"
- If it's truly getting attention: "Multiple groups already — this one's moving fast"
- **Never fake urgency.** If it's slow, pivot to value content instead of lying about demand.
