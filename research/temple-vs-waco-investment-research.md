### Key Takeaway
Temple is in a **buyer's market correction** — prices pulled back after the 2020–2023 boom. This is where investors find deals. Waco is **tighter on inventory** with prices holding steadier, meaning less negotiation room but more stability.

2. **Fort Hood (III Corps)** — 45,000+ active duty, tens of thousands of family members. Single largest economic engine in Central Texas.

1. **Baylor University** — ~20,000 students, top 20 employer in the region

**Temple wins on diversification.** Three independent pillars — military (Fort Hood), healthcare (BSW), and logistics/manufacturing (McLane, Caterpillar) — mean no single employer can tank the market. Waco is more dependent on Baylor University and tourism (Magnolia). If Baylor enrollment drops or the Magnolia tourism wave fades, Waco feels it harder.

3. **BRRRR** to force equity and refinance at lower basis
4. **BAH/Section 8** premium rents above market

**Note:** Homestead exemptions apply to owner-occupants only. Investors do NOT get the $140K school district exemption. This means investor properties pay full freight on taxes — a $4,700–$4,475 annual hit on a $250K property.

Flood insurance is NOT included in standard homeowner's policies. This is an extra $800–$1,500/yr that eats into cash flow. Temple has minimal flood zone exposure.

## GROWTH & UPSIDE

### Temple Growth Catalysts

1. **$700M Rowan Data Center** — Hyperscale campus breaking ground 2025, operational fall 2026. Creates 40 permanent jobs plus massive construction employment.
2. **SeAH Superalloy Technologies** — $110M high-performance metal manufacturing facility, 100+ jobs in aerospace/auto/energy.
3. **$15M I-35/I-14 Federal Upgrade** — Highway capacity expansion, safety improvements.
4. **Downtown City Center Project** — Completed Nov 2025. New parking garage, pedestrian improvements, mixed-use revitalization.
5. **Population Growth** — 3.14% annually, 7% projected through 2029. Temple is one of the fastest-growing cities in Central Texas.
6. **Industrial Spillover** — Austin's eastward expansion pushing logistics and manufacturing toward Temple. Land costs 1/3 of Austin.

**Temple EDC 2024 Results:** $122M invested, 124 jobs created in a single year.

### Waco Growth Catalysts

1. **Graphic Packaging International** — $1B facility
2. **Electrolit** — $400M beverage production plant (2025)
3. **Walmart Manufacturing** — $380M facility in Robinson (adjacent)
4. **$250M I-35 Reconstruction** — Widening from 6 to 8 lanes through McLennan County
5. **TSTC $850M Expansion** — Proposition 1 passed Nov 2025, workforce training campus expansion
6. **Magnolia Market Expansion** — Chip & Joanna invested $10.4M in expansion

### Which Market Has More Room to Run?

**Temple.** Here's why:
- Temple is growing 3x faster than Waco (3.14% vs 1.02%)
- Temple's home prices are in a correction (-3% to -4%), meaning you're buying the dip
- Temple has $810M+ in announced capital projects (data center + manufacturing)
- Austin spillover effect is real — Temple is 65 miles north on I-35
- BAH rate increases (+6.3% for 2026) directly increase rental demand ceiling
- BSW is expanding, not contracting

Waco's growth is solid but more mature. The Magnolia effect has already been priced in. The I-35 widening will help, but Waco's appreciation has been steadier — less room for a snap-back.

## THE WINNER FRAMEWORK

### Best for Cash Flow?

**WACO** — barely, and only because of lower entry price.

At $218K purchase price vs $255K, your debt service is $189/mo less. Rents are comparable ($1,550 vs $1,600). Neither market truly cash-flows at 7.25% DSCR rates with 25% down and full expenses, but Waco loses less money per month (-$541 vs -$739).

**BUT** — if you're buying Temple at the current correction prices ($230K–$240K for a $260K comparable), Temple's effective cash flow matches or beats Waco.

**Verdict: TIE with edge to Waco on paper, Temple if you buy the dip.**

### Best for Appreciation?

**TEMPLE** — clear winner.

- 3.14% population growth vs 1.02%
- $810M+ in announced capital investment
- Austin spillover corridor positioning
- Currently in a correction = buy low
- BAH increases driving rental rate ceilings higher
- BSW expansion anchoring healthcare employment

**Waco's appreciation story already happened.** The Magnolia effect priced in 2016–2022. Temple's data center + manufacturing boom is just starting.

**Verdict: TEMPLE by a wide margin.**

### Best for BRRRR?

**TEMPLE** — here's the play.

- More inventory (5.3 months of supply) = more deals to find
- Correction pricing means you can buy 10–15% below peak
- Longer DOM (86–103 days) = desperate sellers willing to negotiate
- Sale-to-list at 96–97% = 3–4% instant negotiation room
- More distressed/deferred-maintenance inventory in older Temple neighborhoods
- After-repair values are well-established in neighborhoods like Canyon Creek, Windmill Farms, Alta Vista

Waco's tighter inventory (3.2 months) and 98.4% sale-to-list ratio mean you're fighting harder for deals and overpaying on the buy side.

**Verdict: TEMPLE.**

### Best for Turnkey Buy-and-Hold?

**TIE** — depends on your strategy.

- **Temple** if you want appreciation + military/medical tenant demand
- **Waco** if you want slightly higher yield + student housing play
- Both require 25%+ down and 12–24 months of patience for rent growth to bridge the cash-flow gap at current rates

**Verdict: TIE. Pick your demand driver.**

### Best for MTR / Travel Nurse?

**TEMPLE** — not even close.

- BSW Temple is a 640-bed Level I Trauma Center running constant travel nurse contracts
- 100–150 new medical residents arrive every June like clockwork
- Furnished rental premium of 40–70% over market rent
- PGY-1 residents earning $71K make strong tenants
- BSW is THE employer, not one of several hospitals

Waco has hospitals (Ascension Providence, BSW Hillcrest) but no single anchor comparable to BSW Temple's scale. Waco's travel nurse housing supply (174 listings) is adequate but fragmented.

**Verdict: TEMPLE by a mile.**
