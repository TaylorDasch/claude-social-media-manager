## PATTERNS IN HIGH-PERFORMING BUILDER CONTENT

### Title Formulas That Work
1. **Negative/Warning titles dominate.** "WORST builders," "Mistakes to avoid," "Regret traps," "Builders to avoid" — Jeremy Knight proved this: 83% of his top-traffic videos use negative framing
2. **Ranking/tier list format.** "Ranked," "Tier List," "Best to Worst" — creates inherent curiosity and debate
3. **Specific builder names in title.** "What DR Horton's Strategy Means for Buyers" — captures search traffic from people researching specific builders before buying
4. **"Honest" and "Real" signaling.** Viewers want agent who will name names, not generic advice
5. **Location + keyword.** Always include the metro/city: "DFW," "Houston," "Austin," "San Antonio"

### Video Structure That Works
1. **Hook (0-10 sec):** Bold claim or shocking stat. "I've helped 300+ families buy new construction and these are the builders I'd NEVER recommend."
2. **Credibility frame (10-30 sec):** Transaction count, years of experience, certifications
3. **Tier/ranking system (bulk of video):** Either bottom-up (worst to best — builds anticipation) or tier-by-tier with clear visual graphics
4. **Per-builder segment:** 2-3 minutes each covering: build quality, customer service, design options, value, warranty, resale
5. **Honest negatives:** The content that drives the most engagement is the criticism. Don't softball it.
6. **"What about [builder]?" anticipation:** Address common questions viewers will have
7. **CTA:** "If you're buying new construction in [market], here's my number"
8. **Runtime sweet spot:** 12-20 minutes for tier lists, 7-12 minutes for single-builder deep dives

### Thumbnail Patterns
1. Red/yellow warning colors
2. Builder logos with X marks or ranking numbers
3. Agent's face with concerned/shocked expression
4. Text overlay: "AVOID" or "TOP 3" or tier list graphic
5. Split-screen comparing good vs. bad

### What Drives Comments (Engagement)
1. People DEFENDING their builder choice (or attacking it)
2. "What about [builder not covered]?" requests
3. Personal horror stories with specific builders
4. Regional quality debates ("DR Horton is fine in my area")
5. "Can you do a video on [specific community]?"
6. Asking for agent representation

### YouTube SEO for Builder Content
- **Primary keyword placement:** First 40 characters of title (before mobile truncation)
- **Title length:** Under 60 characters
- **Power words that boost CTR by 8.3%:** "ultimate," "complete," "surprising," "proven," "honest," "avoid"
- **Description:** Strong hook in first 2 lines, natural keywords, timestamps, contact info, CTA
- **Tags:** Builder names individually, "[city] new construction," "[city] home builders," "builder review," "best builders [state]"
- **35% of YouTube traffic comes from search** — these are high-intent buyers actively researching

## RECOMMENDED VIDEO STRUCTURE FOR TAYLOR

### Title Options (ranked by predicted performance)
1. "Top Builders in Temple TX RANKED — Honest Agent Review" (37 chars before "RANKED")
2. "New Construction Builders in Temple TX: Best to WORST" (52 chars)
3. "Buying New Construction in Temple TX? Watch This First" (53 chars)
4. "Temple TX Builder Tier List — Which Ones I'd Actually Recommend" (61 chars)
5. "The TRUTH About New Construction Builders in Temple TX" (53 chars)

### Suggested Format
**Runtime target:** 15-18 minutes

1. **Hook (0:00-0:15):** "I've sold $27 million in Temple TX real estate and worked with every major builder in this market. Here's my honest tier list — including the ones I'd tell my own family to avoid."
2. **Why this matters (0:15-1:00):** Temple's new construction boom, how many buyers get burned, why you need a local agent's perspective (not just online reviews)
3. **Tier system (1:00-14:00):** Rank local builders. Use creative Texas tier names (Zak Schmidt's "Brisket Perfection" approach adapted for Temple). Cover: build quality, warranty service, lot selection, incentive games, resale value. Name specific subdivisions.
4. **Builder incentives breakdown (14:00-16:00):** What the current rate buydowns and closing cost credits actually mean. Red flags in incentive packages.
5. **3 mistakes buyers make (16:00-17:00):** Quick hits — not using a buyer's agent, skipping inspections on new construction, not checking builder's warranty history
6. **CTA (17:00-18:00):** "I help buyers navigate new construction in Temple every week. Link in description."

### One Creative Element

Or use a thermal camera / moisture meter on different builder homes to show quality differences visually. This is the Matt Risinger approach adapted for a real estate agent — show don't tell.
