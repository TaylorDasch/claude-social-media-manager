>
> Budget $2,400-$3,600/yr for a standard 3/2 rental ($200-$300/mo). That's $150-$250/mo more than investors from the Midwest or Southeast expect. On a $1,495/mo rent (3BR median in Temple's 76504 zip), insurance alone eats 13-20% of gross rent. It's a real cash flow killer and I won't pretend otherwise.
>
> What's driving it: Texas weather (hail, wind, tornado risk), litigation costs, and reinsurance market tightening. Bell County specifically gets hail storms that trigger roof claims across entire neighborhoods. Insurers price that in.
>
> How to mitigate without lying to yourself:
>
> 1. **Buy newer construction (2015+).** Impact-resistant roofing, updated electrical, and modern plumbing get you better rates. Canyon Creek and Prairie Ridge have newer inventory.
> 2. **Raise deductibles.** Moving from $1K to $2,500 deductible can save $400-$600/yr. You're self-insuring small claims, but as an investor you should be anyway.
> 3. **Shop annually.** Don't auto-renew. Get 3-4 quotes every year. Rates vary wildly between carriers in Texas.
> 4. **Bundle.** If you have 3+ properties, a portfolio policy or landlord package saves 10-15%.
> 5. **Avoid flood zones.** Parts of Temple near the Leon River and Nolan Creek are in FEMA flood zones. That's an extra $1,200-$2,400/yr you don't need.
>
> The math still works in Temple if you underwrite honestly. The investors who get burned are the ones using national averages for insurance instead of Texas reality.
