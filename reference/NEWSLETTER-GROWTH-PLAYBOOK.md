### 3.2 Beehiiv Boosts — Get Recommended by Other Newsletters (Month 4)

**How Boosts work as a GROW publisher (you're paying to acquire subscribers):**
1. Connect Stripe to your Beehiiv account
2. Deposit minimum $50 into your Beehiiv wallet
3. Browse the Boost marketplace for newsletters whose audience overlaps with yours
4. Apply to Boost offers from relevant newsletters
5. Set your cost-per-subscriber budget (typical range: $1-3 for niche audiences)
6. When someone subscribes to that newsletter, they see your newsletter as a recommendation
7. If they opt in, you pay only for verified, engaged subscribers (10-17 day verification window)

**Target newsletters to Boost through:**
- Real estate investing newsletters (national or Texas-focused)
- Financial independence / FIRE community newsletters
- Military finance newsletters (Fort Hood crossover)
- Small business / side income newsletters (investors often overlap)

**Budget recommendation:** Start with $100/month. At $2/subscriber, that's ~50 new subscribers/month. Test for 2 months, measure 30-day engagement of Boost-acquired subscribers vs. organic. If open rates stay above 35%, scale the budget.

**How Boosts work as a MONETIZE publisher (you get paid for recommending others):**
Once your newsletter has enough subscribers (300+), you can ALSO earn money by recommending other newsletters. This creates a flywheel: Boost revenue funds more Boost spending for your own growth.

## BEEHIIV-SPECIFIC TACTICS

### Referral Program — Detailed Setup

1. **Navigate:** Dashboard → Grow → Referral Program → Enable
2. **Create milestones:** Add each of the 5 milestones from Phase 2.1 above
3. **Reward fulfillment:**
   - Digital rewards: Upload PDF directly or provide a download URL
   - Strategy call: Create a dedicated Calendly link (e.g., `calendly.com/taylordasch/investor-strategy`) and send it as a promo code reward
   - "Founding Investor" credit: Manually add their name to the newsletter template footer
4. **Enable the widget:** Add the referral block to your newsletter template. Beehiiv auto-populates each subscriber's unique link and progress bar.
5. **Test it:** Subscribe with a test email. Verify the referral link works, rewards trigger correctly, and the progress display is accurate.

### Boost — When and How to Enable

**When:** After upgrading to Scale plan (Month 4) and having 250+ subscribers with verified engagement (35%+ open rate).

**As a GROW publisher (acquiring subscribers):**
- Start budget: $100/month
- Target CPS (cost per subscriber): $1.50-$2.50 for real estate niche
- Filter by: Finance, Real Estate, Business categories
- Require 30%+ open rate from partner newsletters
- Monitor: Track 30-day retention of Boost-acquired subscribers. If they're unsubscribing at >10% within 30 days, the partner newsletter's audience isn't a match.

**As a MONETIZE publisher (earning by recommending others):**
- Enable once you hit 500+ subscribers
- Only recommend newsletters you'd actually read — your audience trusts your judgment
- Typical earnings: $1-3 per verified referral
- Use earnings to fund your own Boost spending → self-sustaining growth loop

### A/B Testing Subject Lines (Post-Upgrade)

**When to start:** After upgrading to Scale plan and having 250+ subscribers (you need enough data for statistical significance).

**What to test first:**
1. Number-forward vs. question-forward subject lines
   - A: "$347/month cash flow — but I'd pass on this deal"
   - B: "Would you buy this Temple rental? (I wouldn't)"
2. Specificity vs. curiosity gap
   - A: "This 76502 duplex runs 8.2% cap rate"
   - B: "The deal I almost bought this week"
3. Short vs. long
   - A: "Temple TX: 6.4% cap rate"
   - B: "I underwrote 3 Temple rentals this week — one of them actually works"

**Settings:**
- Test audience: 20% of subscribers (10% per variant)
- Test duration: 2 hours (Beehiiv default is 60 min — extend it for a smaller list)
- Winning metric: Open rate
- Auto-send winner to remaining 80%

**Log every test result.** After 10 issues of testing, you'll have a clear subject line formula that works for your specific audience.

### Best Send Times for Investor Audience

**Start with:** Thursday 7:00 AM CT

**Rationale:** Investors check email early. Thursday gives them the weekend to analyze deals mentioned in the newsletter. This is also before the Friday new listing dump, so your analysis is top-of-mind when they're browsing MLS over the weekend.

**Test after 6 issues:**
- Thursday 7 AM vs. Thursday 12 PM (lunch read)
- Thursday 7 AM vs. Tuesday 7 AM (mid-week vs. pre-weekend)

**What the data says:** Financial/investing newsletters see highest open rates Tuesday-Thursday, 6-9 AM local time. Saturday morning is a dark horse for investor audiences (weekend deal-hunting mode). Test Saturday 8 AM as a wild card in Month 3.

## METRICS TO TRACK

### Primary Metrics (Check after every issue)

| Metric | Target | Red Flag | Notes |
|--------|--------|----------|-------|
| **Open Rate** | 45-55% | Below 35% | Investor newsletters typically run 40-50%. Your list is small and warm — aim higher. Note: Apple Mail Privacy inflates this metric. |
| **Click Rate** | 5-8% | Below 3% | Measured on the Deal Autopsy property link and any CTA links. |
| **Reply Rate** | 3-5% | Below 1% | THE most important metric. Replies = relationships = deals. If you get 5 replies on a 200-subscriber list, that's exceptional. |
| **Unsubscribe Rate** | <0.5% per issue | Above 1% per issue | Some unsubscribes are healthy (cleaning the list). A spike after a specific issue means the content missed. |
| **Subscriber Growth Rate** | 10-15% monthly | Below 5% monthly | Compound growth matters. 10% monthly = double in 7 months. |

### Secondary Metrics (Check monthly)

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Referral conversion rate** | 3-5% of subscribers refer at least 1 person | If below 2%, rewards aren't compelling enough or the ask isn't visible enough |
| **Boost CPS (cost per subscriber)** | $1.50-$2.50 | Above $3 = wrong partner newsletters. Below $1 = suspiciously cheap, check quality. |
| **Boost 30-day retention** | >85% | If Boost subscribers unsubscribe >15% within 30 days, stop that Boost partnership |
| **Lead magnet conversion rate** | 25-40% of landing page visitors subscribe | Below 20% = landing page copy needs work |
| **Social teaser → subscribe rate** | Track via UTMs | Which platform drives the most newsletter signups? Double down on the winner. |

### The Metric That Actually Matters

**Deals from newsletter subscribers.**

Track this in FUB with the `newsletter` source tag. Every subscriber who becomes a lead, every lead who becomes a client, every client who closes a deal. The newsletter's job isn't to have great open rates — it's to create conversations that turn into transactions.

**Attribution path:** Newsletter subscriber → replies to an issue → Taylor follows up → FUB contact created with `source:newsletter` → deal pipeline → closed.

**Target:** 1 closed deal per quarter sourced from newsletter subscribers by Month 6. At Taylor's average deal size (~$3,500 commission), one deal per quarter means the newsletter pays for itself many times over compared to zero marketing spend.

### Monthly Review Cadence

Every 4th Friday (or after every 2nd issue), review:
1. Subscriber count vs. target
2. Open rate trend (improving, flat, declining?)
3. Reply count and quality (any real investor conversations started?)
4. Top-performing subject line and why
5. Referral program activity
6. Which acquisition channel is winning (YouTube, BP, TikTok, organic, Boost)?
7. Any unsubscribe patterns (did a specific issue type cause churn?)
8. Next issue plan — what did readers respond to? Do more of that.
