# FILMING STYLE GUIDE
## Quick reference for camera setups, B-roll needs, and production standards

---

## SETUP A: DESK / TALKING HEAD
**Use for:** Deal breakdowns, explainers, market updates, negative hooks, AEO gap videos
- **Camera:** Eye level, slightly off-center (rule of thirds)
- **Background:** Clean, branded (emerald accent items if possible)
- **Lighting:** Ring light or key light at 45 degrees
- **Screen share:** OBS Studio for clean recording with webcam overlay (bottom-right corner)
- **Audio:** Lav mic preferred, shotgun mic acceptable
- **B-roll needs:** Property photos from MLS, Google Maps screenshots, calculator screencaps

---

## SETUP B: DRIVE-THROUGH / POV
**Use for:** Neighborhood tours, commute tests, "day in the life of a neighborhood"
- **Camera:** Dash-mounted (front-facing for POV, passenger-facing for commentary)
- **Audio:** Lav mic (car noise is manageable with good post-processing)
- **Key shots:** Street signs, school entrances, HOA common areas, retail/dining areas
- **Timer overlay:** For commute test segments (show real elapsed time to BSW or Fort Hood gate)
- **B-roll needs:** Slow pan of representative homes, community amenities, nearby commercial

---

## SETUP C: WALK-AND-TALK
**Use for:** Property tours, on-location segments, "boots on the ground" credibility
- **Camera:** Stabilizer/gimbal preferred
- **Audio:** Lav mic (essential outdoors)
- **Key shots:** Front exterior, street view, backyard, notable features, known issues
- **Movement:** Walk toward camera (more dynamic than walking away)

---

## SETUP D: SCREEN-ONLY
**Use for:** Calculator walkthroughs, spreadsheet breakdowns, map analysis, tax formula videos
- **Camera:** Not needed (screen recording only via OBS)
- **Audio:** USB mic, narrate clearly
- **Overlay:** Facecam in corner (optional but increases trust)
- **Post-production:** Zoom in on relevant cells, highlight key numbers with cursor or annotations

---

## BATCH FILMING STRATEGY

Film multiple videos in one session to maximize output:
- **Videos 1 & 2:** Same shirt, same angle
- **Change jacket/shirt**
- **Videos 3 & 4:** Different look
- **Change again**
- **Video 5:** Third look

Result: 5 unique-looking videos from one sitting (3-hour session).

---

## UNIVERSAL PRODUCTION RULES

1. **1080p minimum, 4K preferred** — YouTube favors higher quality uploads
2. **Captions on every video** — CapCut auto-caption, then manual cleanup for proper nouns (BSW, Fort Hood, Bell County, neighborhood names)
3. **Visual change every 15-20 seconds** in long-form (cut, graphic, angle change, B-roll)
4. **Visual change every 7 seconds** in short-form (TikTok/Shorts)
5. **Background music at 10-15% volume** — kill during math/data segments
6. **Lower third with name + credentials** on first appearance
7. **Consistent intro graphic** — 2-3 seconds max, not a full intro sequence
8. **No slow exterior pan from front door** — that format is dead. Hook with striking feature first.

---

## THUMBNAIL RULES

- High contrast (dark background + bright text works for the brand)
- Maximum 4-5 words on thumbnail
- Face showing emotion > no face (but NOT the "agent pointing at house" trope)
- Use emerald (#059669) for positive indicators, red for negative/warning
- Property photo + overlaid number = highest CTR for deal breakdowns
- Split-frame (before/after, vs comparison) = highest CTR for comparisons

---

## SHORTS / TIKTOK PRODUCTION NOTES

- Record vertical (9:16) — never crop horizontal footage
- Hook in first 1 second (bold statement, visual surprise, or on-screen text)
- State target keywords verbally in first 3 seconds
- Mirror keywords in on-screen text in first 3 seconds
- Text overlays for every number mentioned
- Loop structure: last sentence connects back to first
- Save to TikTok drafts natively, publish manually (third-party schedulers get depressed reach)
- Geo-tag EVERY post: Temple, Belton, Fort Hood, Bell County
