# The Temple Insider -- Newsletter Template
## Buyer/Relocator Edition

> **Audience:** People moving to Temple TX, first-time buyers, military relocators, BSW medical families
> **Separate from:** "The Temple TX Investor Brief" (investor newsletter)
> **Frequency:** Biweekly (every other Tuesday, offset from investor brief on Thursdays)
> **Platform:** Beehiiv
> **Voice:** Taylor Dasch -- casual (2/10 formal), data-first, honest, short sentences, contractions always

---

## Subject Line Formula
- Under 50 characters
- Lead with the data point or the tension
- Patterns that work:
  - `[Number]. [What it means for you.]`
  - `[Surprising fact] + [implication]`
  - `The number every [buyer/relocator] should know`
- Examples: "97 days. That's your leverage." / "5.3 months of inventory. Here's why."

## Preview Text Formula
- 80-100 characters
- Tease the rotating feature (Insider Move, Trap Alert, or Honest Comparison)
- Never repeat the subject line. Expand on it or tease the next section.
- Example: "Plus: what savvy buyers are doing with price reductions right now"

---

## SECTION 1: The Real Number
**Word count:** 150-200 words
**Purpose:** One surprising data point that frames the entire issue

### Format:
```
# [The number, big and bold]

[One sentence: what this number is.]

[2-3 sentences: why it matters to you specifically. Context. Comparison to last year or national average.]

[1 sentence: what to do with this information. Practical.]
```

### Tone notes:
- Open casual. "So here's something wild." or "Let me hit you with a number."
- The number comes first. Always. Then the explanation.
- Include the honest angle. If the number is bad for sellers, say so.
- No hedging. No "it depends." Give your take.

---

## SECTION 2: Rotating Feature
**Word count:** 200-300 words
**Rotates between three formats -- pick what fits, not a rigid cycle:**

### Option A: Honest Comparison
Two neighborhoods, two price points, or two strategies compared head-to-head. Real pros AND cons for both. No winner declared unless the data is obvious.

### Option B: Trap Alert
Something that looks like a good deal but is not. Or a common mistake buyers make in this market. Specific to Temple. Name names (neighborhoods, price bands, builder issues).

### Option C: Insider Move
A strategy that is working right now based on current market conditions. Specific. Actionable. "Here's exactly what I'm telling my buyers to do this week."

### Tone notes:
- This is the meat. Go deep on one thing.
- Use real numbers. SP/LP ratios, DOM, price per sqft.
- "Here's the deal..." is a natural opener for this section.
- Break it down like you are explaining it over coffee.

## SECTION 3: Home of the Week
**Word count:** 200-250 words
**Purpose:** One standout listing deep dive for retail buyers/relocators. The buyer equivalent of "Deal Autopsy" in the investor brief -- but focused on livability, not ROI.

### Format:
```
## Home of the Week

[PHOTO SLOT: Property exterior -- full width hero image]
Recommended size: 600x400

**[Address] -- $[Price]**
[Beds]/[Baths], [SqFt] sqft | [Neighborhood] | [School District] | [DOM] days on market

[2-3 sentences: why this one stood out. What makes it worth your time.]

**The Good:**
- [3 key features -- layout, location, finishes, lot, price per sqft vs neighborhood avg]

**The Honest Truth:**
- [1-2 bullet points -- real negatives. Busy street, small yard, dated kitchen, etc.]

**Taylor's Take:** [1-2 sentences. First person. Would you buy this? Who is this perfect for? What would you offer?]

**Monthly Payment Estimate:** ~$[amount]/mo (assuming [X]% down, [X]% rate, taxes + insurance included)

[BUTTON: Schedule a Showing -- dealswithdasch@gmail.com]
```

### Tone notes:
- This is NOT a listing ad. It is Taylor's honest opinion on a specific home.
- Always include the monthly payment estimate -- that is what buyers actually care about.
- Always include at least one negative in "The Honest Truth." No exceptions.
- Rotate the Home of the Week across buyer types each issue:
  - First-time buyer (under $250K)
  - Step-up buyer ($250K-$350K)
  - Higher end ($350K-$500K)
  - Luxury ($500K+)
- Pick homes Taylor would actually recommend, not just whatever is new on MLS
- Taylor selects the HOTW each issue -- he knows what is worth featuring

## SECTION 4: 3 More Worth a Look
**Word count:** 100-150 words total (3 listings, ~40 words each)
**Purpose:** Quick curated picks with honest 1-line takes (in addition to Home of the Week)

### Format per listing:
```
**[Address] -- $[Price]**
[Beds]/[Baths], [SqFt] sqft | [Neighborhood] | [DOM] days on market
Taylor's take: [One honest sentence. Include a negative if there is one.]
```

### Tone notes:
- NOT "featured listings." These are picks Taylor actually thinks are worth a look.
- The honest take is mandatory. "Great bones but backs up to the highway." "Overpriced by $15K but the lot is killer." "Best deal in this zip code right now, period."
- Mix price points. Do not put three $350K homes. Show range.
- Include at least one under $250K if possible (sweet spot band).

## SECTION 5: Market Pulse
**Word count:** 100-150 words
**Purpose:** Quick-scan market data that matters to buyers. Replaces the need for buyers to check Zillow or Redfin for market conditions.

### Format:
```
## Market Pulse

- **[Metric]:** [Number]. [One sentence of what it means for buyers.]
- **[Metric]:** [Number]. [One sentence of what it means for buyers.]
- **[Metric]:** [Number]. [One sentence of what it means for buyers.]

[One "so what" sentence tying the data together into a buyer takeaway.]
```

### Required metrics (pick 3-5 per issue, rotate):
- Median days on market (current + trend direction)
- Active inventory count or months of supply
- SP/LP ratio (overall or by price band)
- Median sale price (current + YoY change)
- Price reductions (% of active listings with a cut)
- New construction permits or starts
- Mortgage rate snapshot (current + monthly change)
- Absorption rate by price band

### Tone notes:
- One sentence per bullet. Number first, context second.
- Include one metric buyers do not usually see (e.g., "New construction permits in Bell County: 47 this month, down 22% from March. Less new supply coming means less competition in 6 months.")
- End with a single actionable takeaway.

- The surprising data point is the hook that makes people click through to the full page.
- Link to the neighborhood page every time.

## SECTION 7: Taylor's Corner
**Word count:** 50-100 words
**Purpose:** One personal/honest observation about the market. This is where Taylor's analyst voice shines -- not salesy, insightful.

### Format:
```
## Taylor's Corner

[3-5 sentences. First person. One observation, hot take, contrarian view, or heads-up.]
```

### What works here:
- A hot take: "I think [area] prices have another 5% to drop. Here's why."
- A contrarian view: "Everyone is waiting for rates to drop. That's the wrong play."
- A heads-up: "Watch [area]. Three projects just broke ground within a mile of each other."
- A pattern: "I've shown 8 houses this month. Every buyer asked the same thing: monthly payment, not list price."

### Tone notes:
- Lead with the insight, not a preamble. No "I've been thinking about..."
- One paragraph. No filler. This should be the part subscribers forward to friends.
- This is the creative heart of the newsletter. Every issue should have one moment of "huh, I didn't think of it that way."

- [ ] Real Number section leads with the actual number
- [ ] Feature section uses real MLS data (no estimates without explicit flags)
- [ ] Home of the Week has photo slot, 3 key features, honest negatives, monthly payment, CTA
- [ ] All 4 listings have honest takes (including negatives)

- [ ] No "whether you're a..." constructions
- [ ] No "in today's market" filler
