## 2. TWO-STAGE CAMPAIGN ARCHITECTURE (THE STRATEGIC SPINE)

**This is the most important section in the document.** It is the single structural advantage over every other Temple agent running Meta Ads, and neither Claude's output nor Gemini's output framed it clearly.

### The Problem Meta Ads Creates for Real Estate Agents

Any ad that promotes home purchases, rental, buyer/seller agent services, or real estate listings must be declared under **Special Ad Category: Housing**. This is non-negotiable. Declaring it imposes these restrictions:

- **Age locked at 18-65+** (cannot narrow to physician working age of 28-55)
- **All genders required** (cannot exclude)
- **15-mile minimum radius** (cannot target by ZIP)
- **No interest/behavior targeting** (no "Healthcare," "Military," "VA Loan," etc.)
- **No standard Lookalike Audiences** (discontinued for housing post-2022 FTC settlement)
- **No audience exclusions** by location or demographic

Sending cold traffic into a SAC campaign is why most Temple agents waste their Meta budget. The targeting is so restricted that the algorithm is basically optimizing against a "local adults who use Facebook" audience.

### The Workaround: Educational Content Is Not Housing

**Educational/lifestyle ads about "living in Temple, TX" do NOT trigger Special Ad Category.** They operate as standard Meta ads with full targeting capabilities.

### The Two-Stage Architecture

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STAGE 1: AWARENESS (NON-SAC, FULL TARGETING)                 │
│ Objective: Video Views / Engagement / Traffic                │
│ Budget: $5-15/day                                            │
│ Creative: Neighborhood tour Reels, market data videos,       │
│           "Living in Temple" lifestyle content, commute      │
│           maps, school district analysis                     │
│ Targeting: FULL interest targeting                           │
│   - Healthcare, nursing, medical school, Mayo Clinic,        │
│     Doximity, American Medical Association                   │
│   - U.S. Army, Fort Hood, Military.com, USAA, VA Loan       │
│   - Income proxy, life events ("recently moved"),            │
│     behaviors ("frequent travelers")                         │
│   - Age: 28-55 (BSW), 20-48 (military)                       │
│ Output: Video viewers → Custom Audiences (25%+ view, 365d)   │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                            │
                            ▼
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STAGE 2: CONVERSION (SAC HOUSING, RESTRICTED TARGETING)      │
│ Objective: Leads (Instant Form) or Conversions (website)     │
│ Budget: $30-40/day                                           │
│ Creative: BSW/Military lead-gen offers (relocation brief,    │
│           BAH calculator, commute map)                       │
│ Targeting: Advantage+ Audience + Custom Audiences from       │
│            Stage 1 video viewers and website visitors        │
│ Output: Leads into FUB → action plan fires within 60 sec     │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Why This Wins
- **Stage 1 builds a warm audience of Temple-interested BSW + Military prospects using full Meta targeting** that Stage 2 can't legally use.
- **Stage 2 retargets that warm audience with housing offers.** The SAC restrictions still apply, but you're showing housing ads to people who already demonstrated interest in Temple — dramatically higher conversion rates than cold housing SAC campaigns.
- **Competitors don't know Stage 1 exists.** They send cold traffic directly into SAC-restricted campaigns and wonder why it's expensive.

## 9. TESTING FRAMEWORK & KILL CRITERIA

### Week-by-Week Testing Plan (Days 1-60)

**Days 1-14 (Learning Phase):**
- Launch only 2 campaigns: BSW Cold, Military Cold. One ad set each. One creative each.
- BSW: 1 ad (BSW-1 "The HR Gap") static image + long-form copy
- Military: 1 ad (MIL-1 "The BAH Math") static image + long-form copy

- Objective: Leads (Instant Form)
- Special Ad Category: Housing (declared for Stage 2 only)
- Advantage+ Audience: ON
- **Do not change anything during Days 1-14.** The algorithm is learning. Any modification resets the learning phase.
- Watch: CPM ($20-$35 healthy), CTR (>0.5% healthy), whether leads arrive. Note, don't act.

**Days 15-30 (First Optimization):**
- Evaluate CPL per campaign
- Evaluate CTR per creative
- Score leads manually in FUB (rubric below)
- Actions:
  - If CPL > $100 after $300 spent: Test new hook/headline (same image, same audience — one variable)
  - If CTR < 0.5% after 5,000 impressions: Test new first-frame image (same copy)
  - If a campaign is working (<$80 CPL, 1+ qualified prospect): Add second creative to that campaign

**Days 31-60 (Testing Phase):**
By Day 30 expect: 15-30 total leads, 2-5 qualified, baseline CPL and CTR.

- Week 5: Test new ad format (carousel vs static)
- Week 6: Test new copy angle (short vs long form)
- Week 7: Test new lead magnet angle (commute map vs relocation brief)
- Week 8: Test Instant Form vs Website Conversion objective

**Do not test two variables simultaneously.** Market is too small, lead volume too low to isolate multi-variable changes.

### Statistical Significance in Low-Volume Markets
- Wait until each variant generates **25-30 leads minimum** before comparing
- At $40/day budget and $50-70 CPL, that's 28-42 days per variant
- Use 90% confidence threshold (not 95%) given volume constraints
- **Hard rule:** Do not kill an ad before $300 spent AND 10 leads generated. Do not declare a winner before 25 leads per variant. Non-negotiable in Temple's market size.

### Lead Quality Scoring Rubric (FUB custom field)
| Criteria | Points |
|---|---|
| Specific start/move date within 6 months | +3 |
| Pre-approved or actively working with lender | +3 |
| Specific neighborhood or price range stated | +2 |
| Has school-age children (BSW) or family (Military) | +1 |
| Phone number provided | +1 |
| Out-of-state area code | +1 |
| Responds to first contact within 24 hours | +2 |

- **Score 7-10:** Hot lead. Same-day manual call. Daily nurture.
- **Score 4-6:** Warm lead. Auto-plan nurture + manual check every 3 days.
- **Score 0-3:** Cold/informational. Full auto-plan, no manual attention for 14 days.

### Metrics That Predict Closings (beyond CPL)
| Metric | How to Measure | Target | Warning |
|---|---|---|---|
| Speed to first response | FUB auto-log timestamp vs form timestamp | < 5 minutes | > 15 min |
| Contact rate | Leads who respond ÷ total leads | > 25% | < 15% |
| Lead quality score | Custom FUB rubric | Avg ≥ 4/10 | Avg < 3 |
| Cost per qualified lead | Ad spend ÷ leads scoring ≥ 6 | < $150 | > $250 |
| Cost per appointment | Ad spend ÷ booked appointments | < $400 | > $600 |
| Lead-to-appointment rate | Appointments ÷ qualified leads | > 20% | < 10% |
| Appointment-to-offer rate | Offers written ÷ appointments | > 40% | < 25% |

### Kill Criteria

**Kill an ad** when:
- CPL > $125 after $400 spent AND 30 days live
- CTR < 0.4% after 5,000 impressions
- Zero leads after $200 spent
- Frequency > 4.0 with no creative refresh ready

**Kill an ad set** when:
- Cost per qualified lead > $300 consistently across multiple creatives
- Lead quality avg < 2.5 over 20+ leads
- Contact rate < 10% over 20+ leads (targeting/offer mismatch)

**Do NOT kill because of:**
- Zero closings at 90 days (normal — cycle is 90-180 days)
- High volume of "just looking" leads (expected; filter with scoring rubric)
- A week with zero leads (normal fluctuation)

### Creative Refresh Cadence
- Frequency > 2.5 in 7-day window = fatigue starting
- Temple's small audience pool = fatigue in 3-4 weeks (not 6-8 like DFW)
- **Pipeline rule:** Always have the next 2 ad variants ready before current ones fatigue
- Refresh first: hook/first frame, headline, CTA copy
- Don't refresh first: audience targeting (stable), campaign budget (stable), landing page (isolate variables)

## 12. RISK, FAILURE MODES & DIAGNOSTICS

### 5 Most Common Ways Solo Agents Burn Meta Money

1. **Sending paid traffic to IDX homepage instead of dedicated landing pages.** Navigation kills conversion. Always use /bsw-relocation or /pcs-temple.

2. **Not declaring Special Ad Category: Housing.** Ads run fine for 2-3 days, then AI flags and restricts. Policy strike. Recovery takes 7-30 days. **Always declare upfront for any housing ad.**

3. **Changing campaigns every 5 days.** Algorithm never learns. CPL stays high. Agent concludes "Meta doesn't work." Algorithm needs 4-6 weeks in Temple's market. Touching early resets the clock.

4. **No follow-up system before spending $1.** Leads at 2am → you respond 3 hours later → 100x drop in contact rate. FUB auto-text eliminates this only if set up before launch.

5. **Evaluating Meta on 30-day return.** No closings at day 30 (normal) → pause → restart in 60 days (learning phase again) → no closings at day 30 again → pause. This kills $4K in 8 months with 3 learning phases and no optimization. **Real estate paid social requires 90-180 day minimum evaluation. Commit or don't start.**

```
Q6: Any actual conversations with leads?
├── No: Auto-text not sending? Check phone formatting.
└── Yes, no appointments: Conversation failing. Record
    next 3 calls. Pitching too early? Not qualifying first?
```

```
├── No: Fix response speed first.
```

### When to Pause vs Push Through

**PAUSE when:**
- Meta account receives a policy violation strike
- Landing page is down or throwing errors
- FUB auto-text is not firing (leads going cold)
- 25+ leads with contact rate < 10% (systemic follow-up failure)
- Personally at capacity with buyer clients — better to pause than poison conversion rates

**PUSH THROUGH when:**
- Zero closings at 90 days (normal, expected)
- CPL $80-$100 (high but manageable; leads arriving, quality improving)
- Several leads went dark after first contact (normal — 75-80% of real estate leads never transact)
- Creative fatiguing (normal — refresh without pausing campaign)

## 13. 🚨 CRITICAL CORRECTIONS (READ BEFORE WRITING AD COPY)

These corrections come from Claude Opus's deep research output and override what the Perplexity playbook says in a few places. The Perplexity playbook is the spine of this plan, but it has two factual issues that matter for compliance.

### Correction 1: Stark Law Does NOT Apply to Real Estate Referrals

**What Perplexity + Gemini got wrong:** Both framed Stark Law as blocking BSW staff from recommending real estate agents, then built "workarounds" around the assumed block.

**What's actually true (per Claude's sourced analysis):** Stark Law (42 U.S.C. § 1395nn) applies exclusively to physician referrals for **designated health services paid by Medicare/Medicaid**. Real estate is NOT a designated health service. BSW employees recommending a real estate agent to colleagues is **not** a Stark Law violation.

**The actual constraint:** It's institutional, not federal. BSW likely has internal vendor solicitation policies that prohibit agents from marketing on campus. The difference matters:
- **Federal law prevents BSW from recommending you:** FALSE
- **BSW internal policy prevents vendor solicitation on campus:** TRUE
- **BSW physicians can legally recommend you to colleagues:** TRUE
- **BSW physician spouses can legally recommend you in their networks:** TRUE
- **Retired BSW physicians, alumni, and independent contractors can legally recommend you:** TRUE

**What changes in the plan:**
1. **Do NOT write ad copy that says "BSW HR can't legally recommend me" as a blanket legal statement.** It's not legally accurate. Instead, say: "BSW HR focuses on employment logistics — they don't provide neighborhood analysis or real estate guidance. That's where I come in." (Ad BSW-1 "The HR Gap" is still valid — just reframe from legal to practical.)
2. **The Extraco lender channel is ONE path, not THE ONLY path.** Also pursue: BSW physician spouse networks, physician moms groups, physician alumni, independent contractor physicians, and the peer recommendation channel.
3. **The one legal constraint that DOES apply:** RESPA Section 8(a) prohibits paying kickbacks for settlement service referrals connected to federally related mortgages. Do NOT pay BSW employees (or anyone else) cash for referrals tied to a federally related loan. Normal relationship-building, content marketing, and non-compensated word-of-mouth are all legal.

### Correction 2: Texas SB 140 SMS Compliance Flag

**What Perplexity flagged (correctly):** Texas Senate Bill 140, effective September 1, 2025, requires certain SMS marketing activity to register with the state and post a $10,000 bond.

**What this means for your FUB auto-text workflow:**
- Auto-text response to a form submission (where the lead opted in by submitting info) likely has different classification than outbound cold SMS
- BUT the law is new and interpretation is evolving
- **Before Week 0 launch, get a 60-minute consult with a Texas real estate attorney on whether your specific FUB auto-text workflow requires SB 140 registration**
- Budget: ~$300-$500 for the consult
- Alternative: Skip SMS auto-response. Use email-only auto-response + manual call-back within 5 minutes. Costs you speed-to-lead but eliminates SB 140 exposure.

### Correction 3: Fair Housing Language Audit

Every ad must avoid language that could be construed as restricting by protected class (race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, disability):

**Watch out for:**
- ❌ "Perfect for physicians" (occupational — gray zone, likely fine but flagged)
- ❌ "Perfect for families with kids" (familial status — AVOID)
- ❌ "Quiet neighborhood for retirees" (age — AVOID)
- ❌ "Great school district for families" (familial status — AVOID)
- ❌ "Safe neighborhood" (can be a proxy for race — gray zone, AVOID)
- ❌ "Exclusive," "prestigious," "executive" (income proxies — risky)
- ✅ "4-bedroom with fenced yard near Belton ISD schools" (property description — fine)
- ✅ "3 min to BSW main campus" (commute fact — fine)
- ✅ "VA-friendly sellers negotiating on price" (loan type — fine)

**Describe properties and facts, not people.**

### Correction 4: TREC Advertising Requirements (Texas-Specific)

- Every ad must display **"EG Realty"** broker name at font size ≥ half the largest contact info in the ad
- Texas SB 2212 (2017) eliminated requirements for license number, "broker"/"agent" labels, or TREC references (voluntary now)
- For social media ads with char limits, broker name can appear on profile page or linked page if readily accessible
- Facebook/Instagram business profiles must link to **"TREC Consumer Protection Notice"** and **"TREC Information About Brokerage Services"** in ≥ 12-point font
