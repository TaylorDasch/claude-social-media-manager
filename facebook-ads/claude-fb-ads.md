# The $100K playbook: Meta Ads for a solo agent in Temple, TX

Taylor Dasch can reach the **$100K net commission target** by converting Meta Ads into a consistent pipeline of BSW Medical relocators and Fort Hood military families — but only with disciplined execution, not higher spend. At current production ($3,369 net per deal), the math requires **30 closed transactions per year**, up from the current pace of ~26. The gap is not volume — it is *consistency*. Meta Ads, properly structured within Special Ad Category housing constraints, can fill the seasonal troughs between PCS waves and residency cycles that create the feast-or-famine pattern. The two chosen audiences — BSW Medical (estimated **7,000–10,000+ campus employees**, 300–400 residents/fellows cycling annually, 800+ physicians) and Fort Hood Military (**36,000–40,000 active duty**, 10,000–14,000 PCS moves per year) — are the only audiences in a tertiary market of this size with enough transactional velocity and identifiable intent signals to sustain a paid funnel. This playbook covers every operational detail from campaign architecture to compliance to kill criteria.

## Why BSW Medical and Military are the only audiences worth buying

Temple's economy runs on two engines, and both produce *predictable, recurring relocation demand* — the single most valuable attribute for paid advertising in a small market.

**BSW Temple** is a 640-bed Level I Trauma Center and the primary teaching hospital for Texas A&M's medical school. The system employs **49,000–59,000 people** across Texas; the Temple campus alone is estimated at 7,000–10,000+ employees. BSW runs **21 residency programs and 26 fellowship programs**, cycling approximately **100–150 new trainees onto campus every July 1**. Match Day falls the third Friday of March (March 20, 2026), giving a precise 90-day window where incoming residents are actively searching for housing. Attending physician recruitment runs year-round but peaks July through December. PGY-1 residents earn **$70,993/year** — enough to buy in the $220K–$280K range with physician mortgage products. Attending salaries range from **$214K** (primary care, Temple-specific) to **$500K+** (orthopedics, cardiology nationally). Nurses average **$85K/year** at BSW Temple. Every one of these people needs housing, and many are relocating from out of state with zero local knowledge.

**Fort Hood** stations **36,000–40,000 soldiers** with approximately **49,000–50,000 family members**. The installation processes an estimated **10,000–14,000 PCS moves annually**, with the primary wave running May through August and a secondary wave in December. BAH rates for 2026 are **$1,695/month** for an E-5 with dependents and **$2,340/month** for an O-3 with dependents — translating to purchasing power of roughly $230K–$340K in the Temple-Belton corridor. The name reverted from Fort Cavazos to **Fort Hood** on July 28, 2025. VA loan volume in Texas leads the nation at 43,000+ loans annually, and **74.1% of VA borrowers put zero down**. The Pentagon's announced 50% reduction in discretionary PCS moves phases in starting FY2027, making the current PCS volume a window that will narrow.

The Stark Law question is resolved: **Stark Law does not apply to real estate referrals**. It governs physician referrals for designated health services paid by Medicare/Medicaid — not personal recommendations for real estate agents. BSW employees are legally free to recommend agents to colleagues. The constraint is institutional, not legal: BSW likely prohibits vendor solicitation on campus, so the pipeline must flow through content, Meta Ads, and lender partnerships rather than direct on-campus marketing.

**The $1,500/month tier is the right starting point.** It provides enough budget for the algorithm to exit the learning phase (Meta needs ~50 conversions per week for optimization; Leads objective has a lower threshold) while allowing split-testing across both audiences. Combined with current organic production at ~26 deals/year pace, adding 10–15 Meta-sourced closings reaches the 30-deal target for $100K net.

**Critical caveat on timeline:** Facebook leads are early-funnel. Average time from lead to closing is **6–12 months**, not 30–60 days. The first 90 days of ad spend should be viewed as pipeline investment, not immediate ROI. ROAS for real estate Facebook Ads averages **0.64** in the short term (Stape.io April 2025 data) — profitability materializes over months, not weeks.

## Funnel architecture: from cold impression to Follow Up Boss action plan

The funnel has four tiers, each with a specific campaign objective, audience temperature, and CRM routing path.

**Tier 1 — Cold awareness (video engagement campaigns).** Upload neighborhood tour videos and market update content natively to Facebook and Instagram. Do *not* run these under Special Ad Category if they are purely educational content about the community — they function as audience-building assets. Target the Temple-Belton-Killeen DMA broadly. Objective: Video Views. Budget: 10% of total. Purpose: Build video viewer custom audiences (people who watched 25%, 50%, 75%, 95% of videos) for retargeting in Tier 2. This is the workaround for the loss of Lookalike Audiences under housing restrictions.

**Tier 2 — Warm retargeting (lead generation campaigns).** Retarget video viewers, website visitors, and page engagers with lead-magnet offers under Special Ad Category: Housing. Campaign objective: Leads (native lead forms). Lead forms should include **3–4 qualifying questions**: name, email/phone, timeline ("When are you looking to move?" dropdown), and a segmentation question ("What brings you to Temple?" with options: BSW Medical/Healthcare, Military/PCS, Other). Budget: 20% of total. This tier converts warm audiences at **50–70% lower cost** than cold traffic.

**Tier 3 — Cold lead generation (primary spend).** Leads objective, Special Ad Category: Housing, targeting Temple + 15-mile radius. Two separate campaigns: BSW Medical and Military PCS. Each campaign runs 2–3 ad sets with different creative angles. Lead forms capture contact info plus the segmentation question. Budget: 70% of total. Use Facebook's native Instant Forms with 2–3 qualifying questions to improve lead quality — research shows adding qualifying questions filters out low-intent leads without dramatically increasing CPL.

**Tier 4 — Nurture and conversion (off-platform).** This is where Follow Up Boss takes over. Leads route to the correct action plan based on form responses. The AI chatbot handles immediate engagement. Action plans deliver drip sequences calibrated to each audience's timeline (residents have a hard July 1 deadline; PCS families have orders with specific report dates; separating veterans may be 6–18 months out).

**Lead forms vs. landing pages:** Use native Facebook Lead Forms as the primary capture mechanism. They convert at **12.54%** (Wordstream) vs. 4.3% for landing pages, and the mobile experience is frictionless. However, for higher-intent offers (home valuation, consultation booking), direct to one of the 44 existing SEO landing pages. The hybrid approach maximizes volume while maintaining quality. Add a "Higher Intent" qualifier: a pre-filled form field asking "Are you pre-approved or working with a lender?" — this filters effectively without raising CPL significantly.

**Lead magnets that convert for these specific audiences:**

For BSW Medical:
- "The BSW Resident's Guide to Temple Housing: Neighborhoods, Commutes, and School Districts" (PDF)
- "What $280K Buys You Within 10 Minutes of BSW Campus" (carousel/video)

## Audience targeting within Special Ad Category housing restrictions

Meta's Special Ad Category: Housing eliminates most precision targeting. No ZIP codes, no age narrowing (locked at 18–65+), no gender selection, no detailed demographics, no income, no education level, **no Lookalike Audiences** (discontinued August 2022; Special Ad Audiences also killed October 2022). Geographic targeting must use a **minimum 15-mile radius** — no exceptions.

**What IS still available:**
- **Location:** City, county, DMA, or radius (15-mile minimum). Target "Temple, TX" with 15-mile radius — this naturally captures Temple, Belton, Salado, western portions of Bell County, and parts of the I-35 corridor. A separate campaign can target "Killeen, TX" with 15-mile radius to capture Fort Hood, Harker Heights, Copperas Cove, and Nolanville.
- **Custom Audiences from CRM data:** Upload the 607 Follow Up Boss contacts to create a Custom Audience. Requires completing Meta's **Customer List Custom Audience Certification** (mandatory since January 2025). Separate lists by action plan: BSW Medical contacts, Military contacts, etc.
- **Website Custom Audiences:** Retarget visitors to specific landing pages (e.g., visitors to the BSW relocation page vs. Fort Hood neighborhood page). Requires Facebook Pixel installed on all 44 SEO landing pages.
- **Video Custom Audiences:** Retarget people who watched 25%, 50%, 75%, or 95% of uploaded Facebook/Instagram videos. Retention window up to 365 days. *This is the most powerful targeting mechanism available under housing restrictions.*
- **Engagement Custom Audiences:** People who interacted with your Facebook page, Instagram profile, or specific posts.
- **Advantage+ Audience:** Meta's AI-driven broad targeting that replaces Lookalikes. Provide audience "suggestions" (e.g., location + a few broad signals) and let the algorithm optimize. Test this against manual targeting — results vary.

**Retargeting tiers (time-based segmentation):**
- **0–3 days post-engagement:** Highest intent. Serve direct consultation offer. "You looked at Temple neighborhoods — here's what's available this week."
- **4–7 days:** Warm. Serve lead magnet (guide download, market report). Social proof creative (testimonials, transaction count).
- **8–30 days:** Cooling. Serve educational content (video tours, market updates). Rebuild engagement.
- **31–180 days:** Long nurture. Monthly market updates, new listing alerts, community content. Low budget, high frequency cap (1–2 impressions/week).

**YouTube viewer retargeting on Meta is not natively possible.** YouTube data lives in Google's ecosystem. Workarounds: (1) Embed YouTube videos on your website pages and retarget those page visitors via Facebook Pixel, (2) Re-upload the same 8+ neighborhood tour videos natively to Facebook/Instagram and build video viewer audiences directly on Meta, (3) Use pixeled short links (tools like PixelMe) in YouTube descriptions that fire the Facebook Pixel before redirecting. **Option 2 is the highest-leverage move** — upload all 8 neighborhood tours to Facebook, run them as Tier 1 engagement content, and build retargeting pools of engaged viewers.

## Follow Up Boss integration and lead routing

The technical integration between Meta Ads and Follow Up Boss determines whether leads reach the right action plan within minutes or rot in an unassigned queue.

**Setup path:** Follow Up Boss has a **native direct integration** with Facebook Lead Ads (Admin → Integrations → Facebook). This auto-imports leads with the Facebook page name and form name applied as tags. The default assigned agent is whoever connected the Facebook account — in a solo operation, this is Taylor.

**Routing to correct action plans:** Create **separate lead forms for each campaign** in Ads Manager. Name them explicitly: "BSW Medical Relocation Form" and "Military PCS Form." FUB auto-tags imported leads with these form names. Then configure **Advanced Lead Flow Rules** (Admin → Lead Flow → click the Facebook source → Advanced Settings) to route by tag:
- Rule 1: Tag contains "BSW Medical" → Assign to BSW Medical Action Plan
- Rule 2: Tag contains "Military PCS" → Assign to Military Action Plan
- Default: Assign to general nurture plan

**For triggering action plans via Zapier:** The native FUB integration imports leads but may not automatically trigger action plans. Use Zapier's **"New Inquiry or Website Event"** action (not "Create or Update Contact" — that one does *not* trigger action plans). This is a critical distinction. The Zapier Premium integration costs $20–50/month but enables custom field mapping and reliable action plan triggering.

**Recommended automation sequence after lead capture:**
1. Lead submits Facebook form → FUB imports within 1–3 minutes with campaign tags
2. Advanced Lead Flow routes to correct action plan based on tag
3. Action plan sends **immediate automated text** (within 60 seconds): "Hi [Name], this is Taylor with EG Realty. I saw you're looking at Temple — I have [specific data point from form]. When's a good time for a quick call?"
4. AI chatbot engages on website if lead clicks through to landing page
5. If no response in 5 minutes, action plan triggers automated email with lead magnet delivery
6. Day 1–3: Phone call attempts (3 attempts, varied times)
7. Day 4–30: Drip sequence calibrated to audience (BSW: neighborhood data, school info, physician mortgage education; Military: BAH calculator, VA loan facts, PCS timeline)

**Speed-to-lead is the single highest-leverage optimization.** Research consistently shows lead contact rates plummet after **5 minutes**. An automated text within 60 seconds, followed by a call within 5 minutes, is the difference between a 30% contact rate and a 5% contact rate. This matters more than CPL, creative, or targeting.

## Testing, optimization, and kill criteria for a low-volume market

Statistical rigor in a market of 85K population requires patience. Meta's algorithm needs approximately **50 conversions per week** to fully exit the learning phase for conversion-optimized campaigns. At $1,500/month, this is achievable with the Leads objective (lower threshold than Conversions) but will take 7–10 days of consistent delivery.

**Testing framework:**
- **Week 1–2:** Launch with 3 creatives per audience (BSW and Military). Let all run without changes. Collect baseline data.
- **Week 3:** Evaluate. Kill any ad with CTR below 1.0% after 1,000+ impressions. Kill any ad set with CPL more than 2x your target after 50+ leads.
- **Week 4:** Introduce 2 new creatives to replace killed variants. Begin retargeting campaigns using Tier 1 video viewers.
- **Monthly:** Rotate at least 50% of creative. In Temple's small audience pool, frequency hits 3.0 faster than in metro markets. When frequency exceeds 3.0, the audience is fatigued — new creative or expanded targeting is required.

**Metrics that predict closings (beyond CPL):**
1. **Lead-to-conversation rate** — What percentage of leads answer a call or respond to a text? Below 15%: check data quality (fake numbers, unqualified leads). Above 25%: targeting and creative are working.
2. **Cost per qualified lead** — After phone qualification, what does a real prospect cost? This should be 3–5x your CPL. Track this in FUB by tagging qualified leads.
3. **Lead response time** — Measure time from form submission to first human contact. Target: under 5 minutes during business hours.
4. **Appointment set rate** — What percentage of qualified leads book a showing or consultation? Below 20%: follow-up script needs work. Above 35%: strong performance.
5. **Cost per appointment** — The single best predictor of downstream revenue. Track this as a custom metric.

**Kill criteria:**
- Ad level: CTR below 1.0% after 1,000 impressions → kill
- Ad set level: CPL more than 2x target after 50 leads → kill
- Campaign level: Zero appointments after 100 leads → diagnose (likely follow-up problem, not ad problem)
- Frequency above 3.0 → refresh creative immediately
- Audience saturation (reach exceeds 50% of estimated audience) → expand geo or shift budget

## Compliance framework: TREC, Meta, Fair Housing, and Stark Law

**TREC advertising requirements for Meta Ads:**
- Every ad must include the **broker's name (EG Realty)** at a size at least half the largest contact information in the ad. Texas Senate Bill 2212 (2017) eliminated the requirement for license numbers, "broker" or "agent" labels, or TREC references — these are now voluntary.
- For social media ads with character limits, the broker name and agent info may appear on the **profile page** or a linked page, provided it is readily accessible and noticeable. If required information would consume more than 10% of available characters, a hyperlink labeled **"TREC DISCLOSURE"** directing to the required information is sufficient.
- The Facebook and Instagram business profiles must display links to the **Consumer Protection Notice (CPN)** and **Information About Brokerage Services (IABS)** — labeled "TREC Consumer Protection Notice" and "TREC Information About Brokerage Services" in at least 12-point font.

**Meta Special Ad Category: Housing — complete rules:**
- **Mandatory** for any ad related to home sales, rentals, mortgage, insurance, or related services. Selecting housing category is non-negotiable — failure triggers ad rejection, account restrictions, or permanent ban.
- **Blocked:** Age narrowing (locked 18–65+), gender selection, ZIP code targeting, detailed demographics (income, education, parental status), interest-based targeting in many categories, behavioral segmentation, Lookalike Audiences, audience exclusions by location or demographics.
- **Required:** 15-mile minimum geo radius in the US. All housing ads are archived in Meta's **Ad Library** and publicly visible.
- **Available:** City/county/DMA targeting, Custom Audiences from CRM lists (requires Customer List Custom Audience Certification since January 2025), website visitor retargeting via Pixel, video viewer Custom Audiences, engagement Custom Audiences, Advantage+ audience optimization.
- **Account shutdown triggers:** Not selecting housing category (most common), circumventing age/gender/location restrictions, using "real estate" or "housing" keywords in non-SAC ads, text-heavy images (over 20% text), personal attribute assertions ("Are you a homeowner?"), misleading claims, landing page mismatches, incomplete business verification.

**Fair Housing Act compliance in ad creative:**
- Describe **properties**, not **people**. "4-bedroom with fenced yard near Belton ISD schools" is fine. "Perfect for families with kids" triggers familial status concerns.
- Avoid any language implying who should or should not buy: no age references, no gender assumptions, no ethnic/religious community references, no disability-related language.
- Do not use exclusionary terms: "exclusive," "prestigious," or "executive" are risky when they function as proxies for income discrimination.
- The DOJ's 2022–2023 settlement with Meta ($115K penalty) established that algorithmic discrimination violates the FHA even without intentional targeting — the platform itself is now monitored by a third-party reviewer.

**Stark Law: resolved, low risk.** The Stark Law (42 U.S.C. § 1395nn) applies exclusively to physician referrals for designated health services paid by Medicare/Medicaid. Real estate is not a designated health service. **BSW employees recommending a real estate agent to colleagues is not a Stark Law violation.** The Anti-Kickback Statute is broader but still limited to referrals for federally funded healthcare items — it does not apply to personal real estate recommendations. The only applicable restriction: **RESPA Section 8(a)** prohibits paying kickbacks for settlement service referrals connected to federally related loans. Do not pay BSW employees for buyer referrals if those buyers obtain federally related mortgages. Normal relationship-building, content marketing, and non-compensated word-of-mouth are all legal.

## Risk matrix and failure mode diagnostics

**The five most common ways solo agents waste money on Meta Ads in small markets:**

1. **Slow follow-up destroys ROI regardless of ad quality.** If response time exceeds 5 minutes, contact rates drop below 10%. At $16 CPL, every unreturned lead within the first hour is $16 burned. The AI chatbot on the website partially addresses this, but it must be supplemented with automated text via FUB within 60 seconds of lead capture and a phone call within 5 minutes during business hours.

2. **Running Leads campaigns with no nurture system.** Facebook leads are 6–12 months from buying. Without an automated drip sequence in FUB (minimum 7 touches over 30 days, then monthly contact for 12 months), the entire investment vaporizes. The 4 existing action plans (Investor, BSW Medical, Military, Luxury) address this — ensure they contain at least 12 touchpoints over 90 days.

3. **Killing campaigns before the learning phase completes.** Allow 7–10 days of consistent delivery before making changes. Do not adjust budgets by more than 20% at a time. Do not pause and restart campaigns — it resets the algorithm. Solo agents are particularly prone to panic-killing campaigns after 3–4 days of no results.

4. **Single-creative syndrome.** Running one static image for weeks guarantees fatigue in a small audience pool. Maintain a minimum of 3 active creatives per ad set, and rotate 50% of creative every 2 weeks. The 8+ existing YouTube videos and drone footage provide raw material for dozens of carousel and video ad variants.

5. **Optimizing for CPL instead of cost per appointment.** A $5 CPL with 0% appointment rate is infinitely more expensive than a $25 CPL with 15% appointment rate. Track downstream metrics in FUB (tag leads by stage progression) and optimize toward cost per appointment, not cost per lead.

**Diagnostic tree: zero leads after 7 days**
- Check ad approval status (housing ads take 24–48 hours)
- Verify Special Ad Category: Housing is selected
- Confirm budget is at least $10/day (below this, impressions are insufficient)
- Review CTR: below 1.0% = creative problem; above 1.0% = conversion problem
- Check lead form: too many fields kills completions (max 4–5 fields)
- Check frequency: above 3.0 = audience exhausted
- Verify Pixel is firing (use Meta Pixel Helper Chrome extension)
- Check mobile rendering: 90%+ of Facebook traffic is mobile

**Diagnostic tree: leads but no conversions**
- Audit response time (check FUB activity timestamps)
- Audit lead quality: are phone numbers real? Add form validation.
- Check CRM routing: are leads reaching the correct action plan?
- Review nurture sequence: is the drip content relevant to the audience?
- Ask: are leads getting a *human* call within the first hour?
- Compare CPL to industry average: if CPL is very low ($3–$5), lead quality is likely garbage — add qualifying questions to the form
- Timeline reality check: Facebook leads close in 6–12 months, not 30 days. If the funnel is under 90 days old, *patience is the diagnosis*.
