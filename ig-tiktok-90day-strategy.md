## 2. Council Debate — Where We Disagree

**Short-form Strategist vs. DR Lead Gen Strategist**
- Short-form says: lean into hooks that travel — "I wouldn't buy this house" style — to build reach and algo trust
- DR says: most viral content brings you Californians asking about Austin. Lean into hyperlocal even if reach caps at 5K–20K views, because the 5 right people in your DMs > 50K views from wrong people

**Ruling:** DR wins at the 90-day horizon. Reach is only useful if it's Temple-shaped reach. You don't need 100K followers; you need 2,000 of the right ones.

**Local Brand Strategist vs. Growth Strategist**
- Brand says: pick one lane (BSW physician relocation), become unmistakable, ignore everything else
- Growth says: Temple is too small for one-lane domination on TikTok specifically; you'll burn out the audience

**Ruling:** Split. IG is the single-lane brand play (BSW-physician + investor authority). TikTok is the broader relocation play (BSW + military + buyer lifestyle) because your own CLAUDE.md rule bans investor content on TikTok anyway.

**Contrarian Critic's objection (valid):**
"Is this even worth doing? Your top lead sources are expireds and BiggerPockets. You could 10x BiggerPockets activity or run a proper expired direct-mail program and outperform 90 days of TikTok. Social media is the slowest lead channel you have, and Temple is too small for viral math to matter."

**Response:** He's right that it's slow. But two things tilt it: (1) BSW relocators are 100% Googling/searching before they arrive — YouTube+short-form captures them when direct outreach can't, (2) short-form feeds YouTube feeds AI visibility (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) and that compounds. Do it **only if you can hold 30 min/day without cannibalizing the revenue channels**.

## 8. Best-Performing Video Structures

**15–30 sec (TikTok sweet spot)**
- 0–2s: Hook (contrarian claim or number)
- 2–10s: Setup (where we are, what we're looking at)
- 10–25s: One concrete point
- 25–30s: CTA or cliffhanger

**30–60 sec (IG Reels sweet spot)**
- 0–2s: Hook
- 2–8s: Stakes ("this matters if you're moving/investing here")
- 8–50s: 2–3 points, each with a visual proof element
- 50–60s: Soft CTA — "DM me 'TEMPLE' for the full list"

**60–90 sec (investor / BSW deep dive)**
- 0–3s: Hook + named property/neighborhood
- 3–15s: What it looks like on paper
- 15–60s: What it looks like when you walk it / run the math
- 60–80s: Your verdict and why
- 80–90s: Specific CTA (DM keyword or link)

**Rules across all structures:**
- On-screen text every 2–3 seconds (retention)
- First frame must include a number or named location (no generic intros)
- Never start with "Hey guys"
- End with ONE specific CTA, never two

## 11. Repurposing Workflow (Honors "Film Native" + "No Longform Repurposing" Rules)

**Direction of flow: short-form → long-form, never the reverse.**

1. **Short-form (TikTok/IG)** captures the hook, the claim, the 30-second version
2. **YouTube** gets the extended 5–8 min version — shot separately, same topic, deeper data, cross-linked in description
3. **Website** gets a blog post version (Yoast-optimized, FAQPage schema, linked to the YouTube embed) — this is where AI visibility compounds. ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini crawl these pages for Temple queries.
4. **Newsletter (Temple Insider / Investor Brief)** pulls the best 2–3 pieces per month into a "what I noticed this week" section

**So the content flow is:**
- Taylor has a take → films a 30–60 sec native short-form → later that week films a YouTube extension on the same topic → transcribes into a blog post (use `transcript-to-blog` skill) → links in next newsletter

This is where the compound happens. One topic → 4 assets. AI crawlers see the topic from 4 angles on your domains. Humans see it on whichever platform they live on.

**DO NOT:**
- Auto-clip YouTube into TikTok (kills native-ness, underperforms)
- Post the same exact video to TikTok and IG Reels (film natively for each — different aspect ratios, different audio norms, different hook pacing)
