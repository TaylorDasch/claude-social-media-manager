**Investor Tip: The real cash-flow math on a Temple TX rental (April 2026)**

Taylor Dasch with EG Realty — pulling from the March 2026 Bell County CAD + active MLS rental comps.

Most investors new to Temple miss two numbers that move cash flow more than list price:

**1. Tax rate. Temple ISD combined rate is 2.25% of assessed value.**
Breakdown: Bell County (0.3128) + Temple City (0.6999) + Temple ISD (1.1372) + ESD1 (0.1000) per $100 valuation, per 2025 Bell CAD. On a $225K property, that's $5,063/year — $422/month — before P&I. Belton ISD runs 2.08%. Killeen ISD 1.99%. Shift 10 miles and the annual tax delta is real money.

**2. 76504 (older Temple) is the LTR entry point.**
Active 3BR rental median in 76504 is $1,495 against $225K median list. Same property type in 76502 (Canyon Creek / Lake Pointe) lists at $300K+ and rents lower relative to price. Cap-rate math favors 76504 unless you're chasing new-build tax treatment.

**All-in monthly on a $225K Temple SFR, 20% down, DSCR 7.25%:**
- P&I: $1,228
- Tax: $422
- Insurance: $125
- PM (5%): $75
- Vacancy (8%): $120
- Maintenance (10%): $150
- **Total out: $2,120 vs rent $1,495 → gap of $625/month**

Temple LTR cash-flow only works under $175K in 76504 — or in 76513 (Harker Heights / Killeen ISD combined 1.82%). The math flips for MTR and BSW-adjacent short-term — different calculation. Full MLS-backed March 2026 update: templetxhomes.net/temple-tx-market-update/
