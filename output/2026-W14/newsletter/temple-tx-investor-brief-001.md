## THE DEEP DIVE: Why Mid-Term Rentals Are the Play in Temple Right Now

The long-term rental math in Temple is getting squeezed. Insurance costs are up 15-20% year-over-year, investor loan rates are still north of 7%, and property taxes on a $250K property will run you $5,600+/year in Bell County. At those numbers, a standard LTR on a financed property in the $200-260K range barely cash-flows.

But there's a lane that most Temple investors are sleeping on: mid-term rentals.

Here's why the math is different.

**The demand floor is Baylor Scott & White.** BSW Medical Center employs 8,884 people. It's the largest employer in Temple and one of the largest hospital systems in Texas. Traveling nurses, medical residents, locum tenens physicians, and contract techs cycle through constantly on 3-6 month assignments. These tenants need furnished housing close to the hospital. They don't want a hotel for 13 weeks and they don't want a 12-month lease.

**The rent premium is real.** A 2/1 on South 41st Street leases long-term at $1,195/mo. That same property, furnished to MTR standards and marketed to BSW travelers, commands $1,850/mo. That's a 55% rent premium for the same roof.

**The objection — "but the rehab costs more."** Yes. An MTR-quality renovation (modern finishes, furnished, washer/dryer in-unit, fast wifi, professional photos) will run $40-45K on a full gut versus $25K for a basic LTR flip. But here's the part nobody talks about: if you're buying a property that needs work, you're doing a full rehab either way. The incremental cost to go from "rental grade" to "MTR grade" is $15-20K — and that $15K buys you $655/mo in additional rent. That's a 13-month payback on the upgrade.

**Who's doing this in Temple right now?** Not many — and that's the point. Furnished Finder shows thin inventory within 3 miles of BSW. The operators who are doing it are charging $1,800-2,200/mo depending on bed count and proximity. There's room.

The formula: buy a sub-$100K property within 2 miles of BSW, full rehab to MTR standards, furnish for $5-8K, list on Furnished Finder + Airbnb (30-night minimum) + direct hospital housing boards. The all-in basis stays under $135K and the rent-to-price ratio destroys anything you'll find in the LTR market.

### THE UGLY (Scars and All)

- **2/1 floor plan.** Most tenants — even MTR travelers — prefer 3 bedrooms. A 2/1 limits your pool to single travelers or couples. If a 3/1 or 3/2 comes up on these same streets at a similar price, that's the better play.
- **1950s foundation on clay soil.** South Temple sits on expansive clay. Foundation movement is not a question of "if" but "how much." Budget $5-8K for piers if the engineer finds issues. This alone could blow the $42K rehab estimate to $50K.
- **Cast iron plumbing risk.** Pre-1960 homes often have cast iron drain lines. A full re-pipe to PEX adds $4-6K.  Get a camera scope before you close.
- **76504 appreciation is slower than 76502.** The growth catalysts (Rowan $700M data center, Meta $800M facility) are on the north side. South Temple is stable, not surging. You're buying for cash flow, not appreciation.
- **MTR management is more work.** Turnover every 3-6 months, furnishing wear, guest communication, cleaning between tenants. This isn't passive. If you're not local or don't have a system, factor in 15-20% management vs 10% for LTR.
