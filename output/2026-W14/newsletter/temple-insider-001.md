## The Real Number

# 97 days.

That's the average days on market in Temple right now. In 2022, it was 31.

Let me say that again. Homes are sitting three times longer than they were four years ago. And most buyers have no idea what that means for them.

Here's what it means: sellers are nervous. A house that's been listed for 60, 70, 90 days? That seller has been paying a mortgage, mowing the lawn, keeping the place show-ready, and watching their phone not ring. By day 60, most of them are ready to talk.

The median sale price is $264,975 right now. Down 2% from last year. Price per square foot dropped from $153 to $147. These aren't massive drops. But combined with 97 DOM? The leverage has completely shifted.

If you're buying in Temple this spring, you're negotiating from strength. Full stop.

## Insider Move: Spring Buying Season Has a Cheat Code

So everybody talks about spring being "the time to buy." More listings, more competition, prices tick up. That's the conventional take.

Here's what's actually happening in Temple right now.

Inventory is at 5.3 months. That's balanced-to-buyer-favorable. For context, anything over 6 months is a full buyer's market. We're close. And the SP/LP ratio across the board is 98.5%. That means the average home is selling for 1.5% under asking.

On a $265K house, that's roughly $4,000 off list price before you even start negotiating.

But here's where it gets interesting. The $200K-$250K price band is the sweet spot right now. Homes in that range are averaging 46 days on market with a 100.2% SP/LP ratio. They're actually going at or slightly above asking. That band is competitive because it's where first-time buyers, military families, and BSW nurses are all shopping.

Go above $250K and the game changes. The $250K-$300K band has the most volume (24.2% of all sales) but sellers are cutting prices to get deals done.

Here's exactly what I'm telling my buyers right now:

1. **Look at the DOM first.** Anything over 60 days, the seller is motivated. Start your offer 3-5% below asking and see what happens.
2. **Ask for closing cost credits.** In this market, $5K-$8K in seller-paid closing costs is not unreasonable. Especially on homes 90+ days.
3. **Don't skip the 60-90 day listings.** Everyone's clicking on the new ones. The deals are in the ones that have been sitting. There's usually nothing wrong with them. They just got priced wrong on day one.

Buyers have more leverage right now than any time since early 2019. Most people just don't realize it yet.

## Neighborhood Spotlight: Prairie Ridge

If you want new construction in Belton ISD without paying Canyon Creek prices, Prairie Ridge is where you look.

Median price sits at $279K. Average days on market is 9. Nine. That's not a typo. Homes move fast here because the price point hits right for young families and military buyers who want something new without stretching past $300K.

It's mostly DR Horton and Stylecraft builds. Solid production homes. Nothing custom, but the floor plans are smart and the finishes have gotten better over the last two years.

The honest thing nobody tells you: lots are tight. Like, your-neighbor-can-hear-your-conversation tight. And there's not much mature landscaping yet because the neighborhood is still building out. It'll fill in. But right now it feels more construction zone than established neighborhood.

Still, at 9 DOM and $279K median, the market is telling you something. Buyers like it here. And with Belton ISD schools, the resale floor is solid.
