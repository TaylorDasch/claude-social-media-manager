### THE GOOD

1. **Hospital proximity is a moat.** Sub-1-mile to BSW, VA, and Veterans hospital. Traveling nurses on 13-week contracts will pay $1,800/mo furnished to avoid a commute. That's a tenant pool that refreshes every quarter.

2. **Turnkey — actually turnkey.** New HVAC, new water heater, new windows, new kitchen. The flipper did the capex-heavy items. You're not replacing the HVAC in year one.

3. **Seller is motivated.** LLC-owned flip, already dropped $14,900, sitting vacant, offering concessions. The listing agent's remarks say the seller "may be open to more work prior to closing for the right price." Translation: there's room to negotiate.

4. **FHA/VA eligible.** If you decide to house-hack or your exit strategy changes, FHA/VA financing opens the door to 3.5% down or zero down. That changes every number above.

5. **No HOA.** Zero additional carrying cost. No approval process for tenants. No restrictions on MTR/furnished rental.

6. **Detached garage with W/D.** Workshop space or additional storage — adds utility for an MTR tenant who needs more than 772 sqft of living space.

### THE UGLY — Scars and All

1. **772 sqft, 2/1.** This is small. Your LTR tenant pool is limited to singles and couples. No families. If BSW ever consolidates or lays off, your MTR demand could soften and you're stuck with a 2/1 competing against 3/2s for long-term tenants at $1,200.

2. **Pier and beam, 1952.** Pier and beam is actually easier to inspect and repair than slab — but on a 74-year-old house, check every pier. Settling, moisture damage under the house, and deteriorated joists are real risks. Get under the house with a flashlight before you offer.

3. **Previous contract fell through.** I had clients under contract on this property. The inspection flagged items. The listing agent says they've been addressed — but "listing agent says" is not the same as "verified by your inspector." Get your own inspection. Period.

4. **$187.82/sqft is steep for South Temple.** You're paying a premium for the remodel. A non-renovated 2/1 on this block might trade at $80-100K. If the MTR strategy doesn't pan out, your basis is high relative to the neighborhood.

5. **Temple ISD, not Belton ISD.** For LTR families with kids, Belton ISD is the draw. Temple ISD is fine, but it's not a selling point. This matters less for MTR (traveling nurses don't have school-age kids locally), but it limits your exit flexibility.

6. **Flipper cosmetics — verify the mechanicals.** New HVAC and water heater are great. But what about the electrical panel? Plumbing under the house? A 1952 home could have original galvanized pipes. The pretty kitchen doesn't matter if the plumbing fails in year two.

### TAYLOR'S VERDICT

Here's the deal on 1807 S 11th.

The long-term rental numbers don't work financed. Period. A 2/1 at $145K renting for $1,200 gives you a 0.76 DSCR — you're writing a check every month to be a landlord. Don't do that.

But the mid-term rental play? That's where this property earns its price tag. Less than a mile from three hospitals. Furnished at $1,800/mo. Self-managed, you're looking at $479/mo cash flow financed or $1,011/mo with cash. The tenant pool — traveling nurses, VA contractors, medical residents — rotates every 13 weeks. You're never begging for tenants near BSW.

The risk is concentration. Your entire thesis depends on hospital demand. If that demand softens, you own a 772-sqft 2/1 in South Temple that's overpriced for the neighborhood as an LTR.

I'd want it at $130-135K to build enough margin for the LTR fallback to break even. At $145K, you're betting the MTR strategy works — and near BSW, that's a bet I'd take.

One more thing: I've been inside this house with previous clients. The remodel is legit — but get your own inspection. Don't trust "the listing agent says the items were fixed." Trust your inspector.
