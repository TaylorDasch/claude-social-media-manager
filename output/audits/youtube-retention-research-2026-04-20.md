## Part 2 — The Realtor Playbook (What The Pros Do)

### The "Living in Method" (Jackson Wilkey, original blueprint)

Small-city realtor YouTube model that generates 40-50 deals/year almost entirely from inbound:
- Anchor channel around "Living in [City]" as the pillar brand
- Target 100-1,000 monthly search volume (not viral volume)
- Relocation content > listing content
- Weekly upload cadence
- Viewers binge 3-5 videos before reaching out — front-load value, don't gate

### The 6-Part Script Structure (Propphy)

Every long-form realtor video should follow this. Taylor's top performer ("Living in Temple 2026") accidentally gets parts right but violates the 0-5 second rule:

| Segment | Time | What Goes Here |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | 0-5s | Motion + bold promise that matches the title. "3 fastest-selling neighborhoods in Temple in 2026." **NEVER "Hi, I'm Taylor."** |
| **Value Preview** | 5-15s | "We'll cover commute, schools, prices, and exactly who each area fits." |
| **Quick CTA** | 15-25s | "I help families relocate to Temple every week — link in description." 10-second max. |
| **Body** | 25s - 80% mark | 3-5 points, visuals, B-roll |
| **Mid-CTA** | 50-60% mark | Soft invite: "Grab the free Temple neighborhood map — link below." |
| **Close + End Screen** | Final 30s | "Watch the Temple Map Tour next" + point to related video |

### The 4 CTR-Proven Title Patterns (from BIGVU data)

1. **"Warning"** in the title — lifts CTR significantly. Taylor has ZERO titles using this word.
2. **Question in title** — drives curiosity clicks. Taylor uses this well ("Should You Move to Temple?" = 499 views).
3. **"Hidden Gems Only Locals Know"** — beats generic neighborhood descriptions.
4. **"vs" comparison** — doubles potential audience immediately. BIGVU reports 100x view potential over listing content.

### Retention Boosters (tactical)

From Retention Rabbit + creator research:

- **Pattern interrupt every 60-90 sec** — change shot, location, props, or visual graphic
- **Captions always on** — raises watch time 8-40% per multiple studies
- **Open loops** — "I'll tell you which neighborhood shocked me at minute 7" — keeps viewers watching to payoff
- **Visual lower-thirds** reinforce claims ("Median price: $285K")
- **Don't introduce yourself upfront** — deliver value first, identity at 0:45-1:00
- **Prop use** (per retention case studies) — holding a map, a chart, or a physical object boosts retention 10-20%
- **Eye contact with camera** — especially on face-to-camera segments. No glancing at notes.
- **Avoid background music over dialogue** — one of your commenters specifically called this out on "Should You Move to Temple"
- **Variable pace** — alternate between on-camera talking head and B-roll walkthrough every 30-60 seconds

### Shorts Retention Specifics

Different rules than long-form. Your Shorts are already crushing (44-52% per VidIQ), so don't overthink this — but lock in these factors:

- **First 0.5 seconds must include motion** (walk, pan, zoom) — static openings are death
- **Text hook appears before voice hook** — viewer reads before they listen
- **Loop design** — end the Short with a visual that loops cleanly to beginning
- **1 line hook + 2-3 lines value + 1 CTA** — script ceiling of ~60 words
- **Never end with "like and subscribe"** — end with the unresolved question or a next-watch link
- **On desktop, add a "Related Video" link** to push Shorts viewers to your long-form (most agents miss this)

## Part 6 — Shorts Retention Tactics (Locked)

Your Shorts at 44-52% retention are your strongest asset. These are the 7 rules to keep them winning:

1. **Motion in the first 0.5 seconds.** Static opener = 30% drop. Walk, pan, gesture.
2. **Text hook on screen before voice hook.** Viewers read first.
3. **Under 60 words total script.** Anything longer compresses pace.
4. **No "Hi I'm Taylor" in Shorts — ever.** Not even once.
5. **End with an unresolved question** OR a "watch this next" visual pointer. Never "like and subscribe."
6. **On desktop after publish, add a Related Video link** pointing to your closest long-form. This is the #1 Shorts-to-long-form conversion tactic most agents skip.
7. **Caption EVERY Short.** Most Shorts play on mute first pass. No captions = no retention.
